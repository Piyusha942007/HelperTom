1.2 The Nature of Handmade — Imperfections Policy
- Handmade and artisanal products are created by skilled craftspeople, not machines. Natural variations, slight irregularities, and unique characteristics are inherent to handmade items and are celebrated as part of their authenticity.
- The following are NOT considered defects in handmade products:
  - Slight variations in color, shade, or dye lot (especially for natural dyes)
  - Minor size variations (up to 5% from listed dimensions for soft goods, 3% for hard goods)
  - Natural material variations: wood grain, knots, stone patterns, leather marks
  - Slight asymmetry in hand-thrown pottery or hand-carved items
  - Brush stroke variations in hand-painted items
  - Variations in weave, texture, or thread thickness in handloom textiles
  - Small bubbles or variations in hand-blown glass
  - Natural fragrance of materials (leather, wood, natural fibers, essential oils)
  - Patina development on uncoated metals (brass, copper, silver)
  - Slight fading of natural dyes over time (a characteristic, not a defect)
  - Minor tool marks on hand-carved wood or stone

- The following ARE considered defects:
  - Structural damage: cracks that compromise integrity, broken parts, loose joints
  - Functional failures: zippers that don't close, clasps that don't fasten, lids that don't fit
  - Staining, mold, or damage not inherent to the material
  - Missing components or significantly wrong dimensions (>10% deviation)
  - Unraveling seams or stitching that compromises the product
  - Glaze defects in pottery that create sharp edges or leaking vessels
  - Incorrect personalization (wrong name, date, message)

1.3 Condition for Returns
- Handmade products must be returned in their original, unused condition.
- Textiles and apparel: Unworn, unwashed, with all tags and labels.
- Home decor: Unused, in original packaging.
- Pottery and ceramics: No chips, cracks, or signs of use. Must be in original protective packaging.
- Jewelry: Unworn, with original packaging and tags.
- Leather goods: Unused, with no scratches, stretching, or handling marks.

1.4 Made-to-Order and Custom Items

- Customized/personalized items with specific color choices, monograms, names, dates, or custom dimensions are non-returnable if produced as per the specifications provided by the customer.

SECTION 5: HANDMADE & ARTISAN FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: The handloom saree I ordered has a slight irregularity in the weave. Is this a defect?
A: No. Slight irregularities in handloom textiles are not defects — they are the hallmark of hand-weaving and distinguish handloom from machine-made fabric. These "imperfections" are celebrated as the human touch of the weaver. Only structural issues like large holes or unraveling threads are defects.

Q2: My hand-thrown ceramic mug is slightly asymmetrical and not perfectly round. Is this normal?
A: Yes. Hand-thrown pottery on a potter's wheel will always have slight asymmetry compared to machine-made ceramics. Each piece is unique. This is the beauty and value of handmade pottery. The listing description typically mentions "each piece is unique" to set this expectation.

Q3: I ordered a hand-painted wall hanging, and the colors are slightly different from the product photo. Can I return it?
A: Minor color variations are expected in hand-painted items due to:
- Paint mixing variations between batches
- Natural dye variations
- Lighting during photography vs. your room's lighting
- Screen calibration differences
If the color palette is fundamentally different (e.g., predominantly blue instead of predominantly red), it may be a listing error. Report with photos in natural light for evaluation.

Q4: The handmade leather journal I received has a natural scar on the cover. It's visible. Is this a defect?
A: No. Natural marks, scars, insect bites, and grain variations are characteristics of genuine full-grain leather. These marks tell the story of the animal's life and are not defects. They are often intentionally preserved by artisans to showcase the leather's authenticity. If the mark is a deep gouge that compromises the journal's structure, that would be a defect.

Q5: Can I return a customized handmade necklace if the name is spelled correctly but I don't like the font?
A: No. If the personalization matches exactly what you entered in the order form, it is not returnable. Font samples are typically shown on the product page. Review them carefully before placing your order. Custom items with correct personalization are final sale.

Q6: My hand-carved wooden bowl has small cracks forming after a week. Is this a defect or natural wood behavior?
A: Wood is a living material that responds to environmental changes. Small surface checks (hairline cracks) can develop as wood acclimates to your home's humidity, especially if you're in a very dry climate. This is natural and not a defect. Deep cracks that compromise the bowl's structure, however, are defects. Report with photos. Proper care (occasional oiling, avoiding extreme dryness/direct sun) prevents most cracking.

Q7: I bought a set of hand-dyed napkins. After the first wash, the colors bled onto each other. What should I do?
A: Natural dyes can be less colorfast than synthetic dyes, and some initial bleeding is possible. Care instructions typically recommend washing separately in cold water for the first few washes. If you followed the care instructions and significant bleeding/damage occurred, report within 7 days. This may be a dye-fixing defect.

Q8: The handmade jewelry I ordered has a slightly different bead pattern than the listing photo. Each piece is supposed to be unique, but this looks quite different.
A: Handmade jewelry, especially beaded pieces, will vary from the photo. However, if the variation is extreme (different bead types, significantly different color palette, different design structure), it may not match the product description. Report with a side-by-side comparison photo. We'll have the artisan review and determine if it's within the expected variation range.

Q9: Can I return a handwoven basket that has a slight smell of natural grass/reeds?
A: A natural, grassy scent is characteristic of baskets woven from plant materials (kora grass, bamboo, water hyacinth, etc.). This is not a defect — it's the natural fragrance of the material and will dissipate over time. Air the basket in a well-ventilated area. A moldy or musty smell, however, indicates improper storage and is a defect.

Q10: My handmade terracotta planter arrived with a crack. The packaging seemed intact. What do I do?
A: Photograph the crack clearly. Report within 48 hours. A crack in terracotta is a defect, whether from transit or manufacturing. A replacement planter will be shipped. Terracotta is fragile, and despite careful packaging, damage can occur. We cover this fully.

Q11: I ordered a custom-sized handmade rug. It arrived 4 inches shorter than what I specified. Is this within handmade tolerance?
A: Handmade rugs have size tolerances. A deviation of 2-3% is generally acceptable (e.g., 2-3 inches on a 6-foot rug). A 4-inch deviation may exceed the tolerance depending on the total size. Measure the rug and photograph the measurement. Report within 7 days. If the deviation exceeds the stated tolerance, a partial refund or remake will be offered.

Q12: The handmade soap I bought has "sweating" beads on the surface. Is it spoiled?
A: Soap "sweating" is usually glycerin dew — a natural phenomenon where glycerin in the soap attracts moisture from the air. It's a sign of high glycerin content (a good thing for skin) and is not spoilage. The soap is perfectly safe to use. Just wipe the beads off. If the soap has an off, rancid smell, that would indicate spoilage and should be reported.

Q13: Can I return a hand-embroidered cushion cover if the embroidery thread color is slightly off from the product image?
A: Thread dye lots vary between production batches, leading to subtle color variations in hand-embroidery. This is a natural characteristic of handmade textile work. Minor shade variations are not defects. If the thread color is completely different (e.g., white thread instead of red), that is an error and eligible for return.

Q14: I bought a handmade wind chime, and the sound isn't as melodious as I expected. Can I return it?
A: Sound quality is highly subjective. Wind chimes produce different tones based on material, size, and construction. If the chime matches the product description and isn't broken, sound preference is not a valid return reason. Listen to sound samples if provided, or read reviews before purchasing.

Q15: My handmade silver earrings caused an allergic reaction. Can I return them even though earrings are usually non-returnable?
A: If the earrings were described as sterling silver (.925) or hypoallergenic and you experienced a reaction, report within 7 days with details of the reaction. If the material is found to be misrepresented (e.g., not actually sterling silver), a full refund will be provided. If they are genuine sterling silver, nickel sensitivity may still affect some individuals. We review these cases with care.

Q16: The artisanal cheese I ordered arrived at room temperature. The ice pack was completely melted. Is it safe?
A: Perishable artisanal foods should arrive chilled. If the product is warm to the touch, it may be unsafe. Report within 24 hours with a photo of the product and melted ice pack. Do not consume if in doubt. A refund will be processed. Food safety is our priority.

Q17: I ordered a handmade wooden toy, and the paint is chipping off on my child's hands. Is this a safety issue?
A: Stop giving the toy to your child immediately. Paint chipping off on contact is a serious safety and quality defect. Report immediately within 7 days. The product will be investigated for non-compliant materials. A full refund will be provided, and the product may be recalled from the platform.

Q18: Can I return a handmade candle if the fragrance isn't as strong as I expected when burning?
A: Candle fragrance throw depends on room size, burn time, and wax type. Soy and beeswax candles typically have a subtler scent than paraffin. This is a characteristic, not a defect. If the candle has absolutely no scent even in a small room after a 2-hour burn, report within 7 days. We'll review with the artisan.

Q19: My hand-block printed kurta has slight print misalignment at the seams. Is this a defect?
A: Minor misalignment at seams is common in hand-block printing and is part of the craft's character. However, if the misalignment is severe (patterns off by more than half an inch or clearly jarring), it may be a quality issue. Report with photos. Our team will compare against the artisan's standard quality benchmarks.

Q20: I bought a handmade pottery dinner set. One plate has a small glaze pinhole. Is this a defect?
A: Tiny glaze pinholes (small dots where glaze didn't fully cover) are common in handmade pottery and are considered part of the artisanal character. They don't affect functionality. If the pinhole is large enough to expose the porous clay body (which could absorb food/liquid), it's a functional defect. Report with a close-up photo if concerned.

Q21: Can I return a handmade paper journal if the paper is rougher than I expected for fountain pen use?
A: Handmade paper has a unique texture that varies from machine-made paper. The paper's suitability for specific writing instruments (fountain pens, markers) should be checked in the product description. If "fountain pen friendly" was claimed and the paper feathers or bleeds significantly, report within 7 days. General paper texture preference is not a return reason.

Q22: My handwoven basket is unravelling at the rim after a week of light use. Is this covered?
A: Yes. Unraveling of the weave under light use indicates a structural defect in the weaving or finishing. Report within 7 days with photos. A replacement or refund will be provided. Handwoven items should be durable for their intended use.

Q23: I ordered a custom pet portrait (hand-painted). The artist captured my dog's markings incorrectly. What can I do?
A: Custom artwork is made from the reference photos you provide. If the markings are inaccurate based on your photos, report immediately upon receiving the digital preview or physical artwork. The artist will make revisions. If the final piece still doesn't reasonably match your reference, a partial refund or full refund will be discussed based on the artist's revision policy (stated on the product page).

Q24: The handmade macrame plant hanger I bought is a different length than stated, and my plant hangs too low. Can I return it?
A: Yes. A significant length deviation from the stated dimensions is a defect. Measure the hanger and photograph it against a tape measure. Report within 7 days. A replacement with the correct length or a refund will be provided.

Q25: I bought handmade incense sticks, and they produce more smoke than I'm comfortable with. Is this returnable?
A: Hand-rolled incense produces varying amounts of smoke depending on the natural ingredients, binder, and rolling density. Smoke level is a characteristic, not a defect. If the incense doesn't burn at all, burns too fast, or has an acrid/burning smell instead of the intended fragrance, that's a quality issue. Opened incense packs are non-returnable for smoke preference.

Q26: My handmade quilt has uneven stitching in some places. The stitching is functional but not perfectly straight. Is this a defect?
A: Uneven or slightly wandering stitching lines are characteristic of hand-stitched quilts and are part of the human touch that makes them special. As long as the stitching is secure and the quilt is structurally sound, it's not a defect. Machine-like perfection should not be expected from hand-stitched items.

Q27: I received a handmade copper water bottle with a dent. Can I return it?
A: Yes. A dent is physical damage and a defect, whether from manufacturing or transit. Photograph the dent and report within 48 hours. A replacement bottle will be shipped. Handmade copper items should be smooth and dent-free when new.

Q28: The hand-dyed yarn I ordered has knots in the skein. Is this normal?
A: Knots in yarn skeins are common across both handmade and commercial yarns. Typically 1-2 knots per skein is considered acceptable in the industry. Excessive knots (3+ in a single skein) is a quality issue. Report within 7 days if there are an excessive number of knots.

Q29: Can I return an artisan-made face mask if the ear loops are too tight?
A: Yes, if the mask is unworn and in its original packaging. Ear loop tension affects fit and comfort, and an unworn mask can be returned within 7 days. Once worn, face masks are non-returnable for hygiene.

Q30: I love the handmade philosophy, but my product arrived with a minor defect that I'm willing to live with. Can I get a partial refund instead of returning?
A: Yes, for minor cosmetic defects that don't affect functionality, you can request a partial refund instead of a full return. Contact support with photos of the defect and mention you'd prefer a partial refund. We'll work with the artisan to offer a fair partial refund. This reduces waste and supports the artisan while acknowledging the imperfection.
