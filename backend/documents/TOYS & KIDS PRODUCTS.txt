SECTION 1: RETURNS & REPLACEMENT POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Toys and Games: 7 days from the date of delivery.
- Kids Apparel, Footwear, and Accessories: 7 days (follows Fashion & Clothing / Footwear policies).
- Baby Gear (Strollers, Car Seats, Cribs, High Chairs): 10 days from delivery.
- Diapers and Baby Care Products: 7 days (unopened packs only; opened packs non-returnable for hygiene).
- Feeding Products (Bottles, Sippers, Breast Pumps, Sterilizers): 7 days (unopened for hygiene-sensitive items).
- Educational and STEM Toys: 7 days.
- Outdoor Play Equipment (Swings, Slides, Trampolines): 10 days.
- Ride-On Toys and Battery-Operated Vehicles: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects).

1.2 Condition for Returns
- Toys must be unused, with original packaging, all accessories, and instruction manuals.
- Battery-operated toys: Batteries (if included) must be removed from the compartment if they were installed. Leaked battery acid damage is not returnable.
- Board games and puzzles: Must have all pieces, cards, and components. Missing pieces discovered after opening must be reported within 24 hours for replacement, not a full return.
- Puzzles: Once assembled or partially assembled, non-returnable.
- Art and craft kits: All materials must be unused and sealed.
- Building blocks and construction sets: All pieces must be present. Opened bags with missing pieces are non-returnable; report missing pieces for replacement.
- Stuffed animals and plush toys: Must have tags attached and be in clean, unused condition.
- Dolls and action figures: Must be unopened or, if opened for inspection, all accessories, clothes, and packaging included. Play wear is rejected.
- Outdoor play equipment: Must be unassembled in original packaging. Partial assembly for inspection is acceptable; full assembly makes it non-returnable except for defects.
- Ride-on cars and bikes: Replacement Only for defects. Change-of-mind returns not accepted once battery has been charged or vehicle assembled.

1.3 Non-Returnable Kids Products
- Diapers and training pants: Opened packs (hygiene).
- Baby wipes: Opened packs.
- Feeding bottles and nipples: Opened packaging (hygiene).
- Teethers and pacifiers: Opened packaging (hygiene).
- Breast pump accessories: Opened packaging (hygiene).
- Kids' cosmetics and bath products: Opened packaging.
- Swim diapers and swimwear: Opened packaging.
- Personalized toys (name-engraved, custom-made): Non-returnable.
- Downloadable content, digital games, and in-game currency codes: Non-returnable once accessed.
- Consumable art supplies (clay, paint, slime): Opened packages.

1.4 Defective Product Policy
- Common defects covered:
  - Battery-operated toys that don't function with fresh batteries
  - Remote-controlled toys with connectivity issues (test within 3 feet, no interference)
  - Broken parts upon unboxing
  - Missing pieces from sealed boxes
  - Paint peeling, sharp edges, or small parts breaking off (safety concern)
  - Electronic learning toys with non-functional screens, speakers, or buttons
  - Board games with misprinted cards or boards
  - Puzzles with duplicate or missing pieces
  - Stuffed toys with torn seams or loose stuffing out of the box
  - Ride-on vehicles that don't move after full charge per manual

- Not covered:
  - Broken toys from rough play, drops, or misuse
  - Water damage to non-waterproof toys
  - Battery leakage from leaving batteries in the toy for extended periods
  - Normal wear and tear
  - Fading colors from sun exposure
  - Missing pieces discovered weeks after purchase (report within 24-48 hours)
  - Remote control range issues beyond the advertised range
  - Toys that the child simply doesn't like or lost interest in

1.5 Baby Gear (Large Items) Specifics
- Strollers, car seats, cribs, high chairs, and playpens are delivered with Open-Box inspection.
- Safety is paramount: Any structural defect, sharp edge, or malfunctioning safety feature (harness buckle, brake) must be reported immediately. These claims are prioritized.
- Car seats with any sign of use or installation are non-returnable for change of mind. This is for child safety — used car seats may have invisible stress damage.
- Assembly of baby furniture (cribs) should be done by an adult following the manual precisely. Self-assembly damage is not covered.

1.6 Safety and Recall Information
- All toys sold on HelperTom comply with BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) safety standards for toys.
- In the event of a manufacturer safety recall, affected customers will be notified via email and SMS. A full refund or safe replacement will be provided, and the product will be picked up at no cost.
- Customers who discover a potential safety issue not covered by a recall can report it to HelperTom. Reports are investigated and escalated to the manufacturer.

1.7 Refund Timelines
- Toys and Games: 5-7 business days after quality check.
- Baby Gear: 7-10 business days after pickup and inspection.
- Defective products (confirmed via photo/video): Replacement shipped within 3-5 business days.

SECTION 4: TOYS & KIDS FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: I bought a board game, opened it, and a crucial game piece is missing. Can I return the whole game?
A: If a piece is missing from a sealed new game, report within 24 hours with a photo of all the pieces laid out showing the missing one. We will ship a replacement game. You don't need to return the opened game; you can keep it, and we'll send a complete new copy, or in some cases just the missing piece if available.

Q2: My child's new remote control car worked for 5 minutes and now won't move. What should I do?
A: First, replace the batteries in both the car and the remote with fresh alkaline batteries. Check that the remote is paired correctly (some require a pairing sequence). If it still doesn't work, report within 7 days with a video showing the issue. A replacement will be shipped. Do not open the car's chassis to inspect internally, as this voids the return.

Q3: Can I return a stroller that I assembled and pushed around the house once, but now find it too bulky for my car boot?
A: Strollers can be returned within 10 days if they are in like-new condition with all packaging and accessories. Light indoor testing is acceptable. However, if the stroller shows any signs of outdoor use (dirt on wheels, scuffs), it will be rejected. Size suitability is the customer's responsibility to check before ordering.

Q4: I ordered a doll, and the doll's hair is already falling out when I took it out of the box. Is this normal?
A: Minor shedding of doll hair when first brushed is normal. However, excessive shedding or bald patches are a defect. Report within 7 days with a photo showing the hair loss. A replacement doll will be shipped.

Q5: My toddler received a plush toy, and the stuffing is coming out from a seam. Is this a safety issue?
A: Yes. Loose stuffing is a choking hazard for young children and a clear defect. Stop giving the toy to the child. Report immediately within 7 days. We will replace the toy with priority. Do not attempt to sew it up, as this voids the return.

Q6: Can I return a box of building blocks if my child doesn't like the colors?
A: No. Once opened and played with, building blocks are non-returnable. Color preferences are subjective and should be considered before purchase. The product images display the color palette.

Q7: I bought a car seat, and the harness buckle is very stiff and hard to release. Is this defective or just needs breaking in?
A: Harness buckles on new car seats can be stiff initially. However, if the buckle is jammed and cannot be released with reasonable adult force, it is a safety defect. Report immediately within 10 days. A replacement car seat will be sent. A functioning harness is critical for child safety.

Q8: What is the return policy for a trampoline that I set up and my kids used for a weekend but now has a torn safety net?
A: If the safety net tore during normal first uses, it is a manufacturing defect. Report within 10 days with photos. If the net tore from misuse (e.g., too many children, sharp objects), it is not covered. Stop using the trampoline until the net is replaced.

Q9: I ordered a puzzle, and when I opened it, the picture on the box doesn't match the puzzle pieces inside. What do I do?
A: This is a packaging error. Report within 7 days with a photo showing the box image and the mismatched puzzle pieces. A replacement with the correct puzzle will be shipped.

Q10: Can I return an opened pack of diapers because they don't fit my baby well?
A: No. Opened diaper packs cannot be returned for hygiene reasons. We recommend buying a smaller pack first to test fit before committing to bulk purchases. Size guides with weight ranges are provided on all diaper product pages.

Q11: My son's new action figure had a broken arm straight out of the sealed box. Can I get a replacement?
A: Yes. A broken figure in a sealed box is a manufacturing defect. Photograph the broken arm and the packaging. Report within 7 days. A replacement will be shipped. Keep the original packaging for the return pickup.

Q12: Can I return a kids' study table if it wobbles after I assembled it?
A: First, check that all screws are tightened and the floor is level. If it still wobbles, report within 10 days. This may be a manufacturing defect. If self-assembly error is the cause, a technician can be sent (charges apply). If it's a defect, replacement parts or a full replacement will be provided.

Q13: I bought a slime kit for my niece, but the slime is dried out and crumbly in the sealed container. Is this returnable?
A: Yes. Dried-out slime in a sealed container is a defect (likely an air leak in the packaging). Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement kit will be shipped.

Q14: The battery-operated toy bike I ordered for my son's birthday won't charge. The charger light stays green. What do I do?
A: Test the charger with a multimeter if possible, or try a different compatible charger. If the battery is confirmed not charging, report within 7 days. This is a defect. Do not attempt to open the battery compartment. A replacement vehicle or battery will be provided based on the diagnosis.

Q15: Can I return a science experiment kit if my child found it boring?
A: No. Content engagement is subjective. Opened kits with used or missing materials are non-returnable. Read the kit description and age recommendation before purchasing.

Q16: I received a stuffed bear with a missing eye. It's a safety hazard for my baby. What should I do?
A: Report immediately. A missing eye on a stuffed toy is both a defect and a choking hazard for infants. Photograph the toy. A replacement will be sent with priority. Dispose of the defective toy safely.

Q17: My daughter's new scooter has a front wheel that doesn't spin freely. Can I return it?
A: First, check if the axle bolt is over-tightened (common in shipping). Loosen it slightly. If the wheel still doesn't spin, report within 7 days with a video. A replacement scooter or wheel assembly will be sent.

Q18: Can I return a Monopoly game because the board is printed off-center and cuts off some property names?
A: Yes. A misprinted board is a clear manufacturing defect. Report within 7 days with a photo showing the off-center print. A replacement game will be shipped.

Q19: I bought water balloons for a party, and half of them have holes and don't fill up. What now?
A: If you tested a sample and found a high defect rate, report within 7 days with a photo/video. We will refund or replace the pack. You don't need to test all balloons.

Q20: What if my baby has an allergic reaction to a teether I bought?
A: Stop using the teether immediately. Document the reaction and report to HelperTom support. We will review the material specifications and batch. A refund will be processed. If the product is found to have non-compliant materials, it will be delisted.

Q21: I bought a kids' swimming pool, and the inflatable ring has a slow leak. I can hear air hissing. Is it defective?
A: Yes, a leak in a new inflatable pool is a defect. Report within 7 days. Locate the leak area (soapy water test) and photograph if visible. A replacement will be shipped. Small pin-hole leaks are a manufacturing defect, not user damage.

Q22: Can I exchange a kids' bicycle for a larger size?
A: If the bicycle is unassembled, unused, and in original packaging, a size exchange is possible within 7 days. Once assembled or ridden, it is non-returnable for size issues. Measure your child's inseam and compare with the bike size chart before ordering.

Q23: My magnetic building tiles have weak magnets and the structures keep collapsing. Is this a defect?
A: If the magnets are significantly weaker than expected for the brand (compare with known genuine tiles), report within 7 days. Weak magnets can be a sign of counterfeit or a defective batch. We will verify and replace if confirmed.

Q24: I received a kids' tent that has a strong chemical/plastic smell. Is it safe?
A: New tents often have a "new product" smell that dissipates with airing out. Air it outside for 24-48 hours. If the strong smell persists and causes headaches or eye irritation, it may indicate non-compliant materials. Report within 7 days. Safety concerns are taken seriously and investigated.

Q25: Can I return a video game if my child finished it in one day and is now bored?
A: No. Physical video game discs are returnable within 7 days only if unopened (shrink-wrap intact). Once opened, games are non-returnable for content reasons. Digital game codes are non-returnable once accessed.

Q26: My kid's play-doh set has dried-out dough in sealed tubs. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. Dried-out dough in sealed containers is a defect from packaging or age. Report within 7 days with photos. A replacement set will be shipped.

Q27: I bought a baby monitor, but the video feed keeps disconnecting. What are my options?
A: Baby monitors fall under the Electronics policy. Check for Wi-Fi interference, distance, and walls. If disconnections persist within the advertised range, report within 7 days. A replacement will be shipped after troubleshooting.

Q28: Can I return a costume that my child wore for Halloween once and now has a small tear?
A: No. Costumes that have been worn, even once, show signs of use and are non-returnable. Tears from wearing are considered normal use, not a manufacturing defect.

Q29: The crayons I bought for my toddler are breaking every time they color. Is this normal?
A: Crayons breaking with normal coloring pressure is not normal and indicates poor quality or a defective batch. Report within 7 days with photos of the broken crayons. A replacement or refund will be provided.

Q30: I ordered a kids' watch as a gift. The gift wrapping is damaged, but the watch is fine. Can I get just the wrapping redone?
A: Report the damaged gift wrap with photos within 48 hours. We can provide a partial refund of the gift wrapping charge or send a replacement gift wrap kit. Since the product is fine, a full return isn't necessary.
