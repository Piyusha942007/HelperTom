SECTION 1: RETURNS & REFUND POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Reusable and Zero-Waste Products (Bamboo Toothbrushes, Metal Straws, Beeswax Wraps, Silicone Bags): 7 days from delivery.
- Eco-Friendly Apparel and Textiles (Organic Cotton, Hemp, Bamboo Fabric): 7 days.
- Sustainable Home Goods (Bamboo Furniture, Cork Products, Recycled Material Decor): 7 days.
- Solar-Powered Products and Energy-Saving Devices: 7 days (Replacement Only for electronics defects).
- Biodegradable and Compostable Products: 7 days.
- Reusable Menstrual Products (Menstrual Cups, Cloth Pads): Non-returnable once packaging opened (hygiene).
- Natural Cleaning and Personal Care Products: 7 days (unopened products only).
- Plantable Products (Seed Paper, Plantable Stationery): 7 days (unused, unplanted condition).
- Refillable and Concentrate Products: 7 days (unopened).
- Upcycled and Recycled Art and Decor: 10 days.
- Second-Hand and Pre-Loved Sustainable Goods: 7 days (condition as described).

1.2 The Sustainability Commitment
- HelperTom is committed to reducing the environmental impact of e-commerce.
- Our Sustainability Policy outlines the standards products must meet to be listed in the Sustainable / Eco-Friendly category.
- Products in this category are verified for sustainability claims (materials, manufacturing process, packaging, end-of-life).
- Greenwashing (false or exaggerated sustainability claims) is strictly prohibited. Products found to have misleading claims will be delisted, and affected customers will receive a full refund.

1.3 Condition for Returns
- General Condition: Products must be in unused, original condition with all packaging.
- Reusable Food and Drink Products (bottles, straws, containers, wraps): Must be unused, unwashed. Once used with food or beverages, they are non-returnable for hygiene reasons.
- Eco-Friendly Apparel: Unworn, unwashed, with tags attached. Follows the Fashion & Clothing return policy but with added consideration for natural fiber variations.
- Bamboo and Wooden Products: Natural grain and color variations are characteristics, not defects (see Section 1.4).
- Solar Products and Electronics: Replacement Only for defects. Follows the Electronics & Gadgets policy.
- Compostable Products: Must be unused. Products that have begun decomposition due to moisture exposure are considered used.
- Reusable Menstrual Products: Non-returnable once the hygiene seal is broken. This includes menstrual cups, cloth pads, period underwear.
- Natural Cleaning Products: Unopened only. Once the seal is broken, non-returnable due to safety and contamination concerns.
- Plantable Products: Must have intact seeds. Planted products (seed paper with seeds removed/germinated) are non-returnable.
- Refillable Products: The initial purchase container is returnable within 7 days if unused. Refill packs are non-returnable once opened.
- Upcycled/Recycled Products: Due to their nature, these may have unique characteristics. The condition is as shown and described in the individual listing. Returns accepted within 10 days if the product does not match the listing description or is damaged upon arrival.

1.4 Natural Variations — The "Imperfectly Perfect" Standard
Sustainable and eco-friendly products, especially those made from natural, recycled, or upcycled materials, inherently possess unique characteristics. The following are NOT considered defects:
- Color variations in natural dyes (plant-based dyes vary between batches)
- Wood grain, knots, and color variations in bamboo and reclaimed wood products
- Texture variations in hemp, organic cotton, and other natural fiber textiles
- Slight size variations in handmade natural products (up to 5%)
- Patina development on copper, brass, and uncoated metal products
- Natural fragrance of materials (bamboo, cork, wool, natural rubber)
- Slight variations in recycled material color or pattern
- Minor imperfections in upcycled products (part of their story and character)
- Variations in handmade seed paper texture and seed distribution

1.5 Defective Product Policy
- Defects covered:
  - Structural damage: cracks in bamboo products, broken cork items, torn fabric
  - Functional failure: reusable zipper bags that don't seal, spray nozzles that don't work, solar lights that don't illuminate
  - Contamination or mold on natural products upon arrival
  - Leaking bottles or containers (not caused by user)
  - Incorrect product delivered (wrong size, color, material)
  - Products not meeting the stated sustainability certifications (e.g., claimed GOTS-certified but no label)
  - Misrepresented material (e.g., claimed 100% bamboo but contains plastic)

- Defects NOT covered:
  - Natural variations as listed in Section 1.4
  - Wear and tear from use
  - Bamboo products splitting due to extreme dryness (bamboo needs occasional oiling)
  - Rust on metal products left in water or humid conditions
  - Fading of natural dyes with washing and sun exposure (a natural characteristic)
  - Mold developing after delivery due to improper storage by the customer
  - Degradation of compostable products after the intended use period (they're designed to break down)

1.6 Refund Timelines
- Standard returns: 5-7 business days after quality check.
- Defective sustainable electronics: Replacement in 5-7 business days, or refund in 7-10 business days.
- Refunds are processed to the original payment method.

SECTION 5: SUSTAINABLE / ECO-FRIENDLY FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: I bought a bamboo toothbrush. The bristles are falling out after the first use. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. Bristle shedding after first use indicates a manufacturing defect (poor bristle anchoring). Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement toothbrush will be provided. Bamboo toothbrushes should be durable for their typical 3-month use life.

Q2: My reusable silicone food storage bag doesn't seal properly. Food leaks out. Is it defective?
A: First, ensure the sealing strip is clean and free of any food particles, and that the bag is not overfilled. If the seal still doesn't close properly on an empty, clean bag, it's a defect. Report within 7 days with a video showing the seal failing. A replacement will be shipped.

Q3: The organic cotton t-shirt I ordered has a slight earthy smell. Is this normal or a defect?
A: A mild earthy or "natural" scent is common in organic cotton and natural fiber products, especially those undyed or naturally dyed. This comes from the absence of harsh chemical finishes and bleaches. It dissipates with airing and washing. This is not a defect — it's the smell of natural fabric.

Q4: Can I return a menstrual cup if I opened the box to look at the size but haven't used it?
A: If the cup is still in its sealed inner packaging (typically a cloth bag or sealed pouch) and only the outer box was opened, it may be returnable. If the cup itself has been removed from its sterile packaging, it is non-returnable for hygiene reasons, even if unused. Check the specific product's packaging layers.

Q5: I bought a beeswax wrap, and it has a slightly sticky/oily feel. Is it supposed to be like this?
A: Yes. Beeswax wraps are naturally slightly tacky/sticky due to the beeswax, jojoba oil, and resin coating. This tackiness is what allows them to seal and stick to bowls and food. It is not a defect. The stickiness reactivates with the warmth of your hands. If the wrap is dry and not sticky at all, that's a defect.

Q6: My bamboo cutlery set has a few small splinters on the fork tines. Is this safe?
A: No. Splinters on bamboo cutlery are a manufacturing defect (insufficient sanding) and a safety hazard (splinters in mouth/food). Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement set will be shipped. Do not use splintered cutlery.

Q7: Can I return a solar-powered garden light if it only stays lit for 2 hours instead of the advertised 8 hours?
A: Solar light runtime depends on the amount of direct sunlight received during the day. A cloudy day or a shaded installation location will reduce runtime significantly. Ensure the solar panel is clean and in a location that receives 6-8 hours of direct sun. If the runtime is still severely short in optimal conditions, report within 7 days. The battery or solar panel may be defective.

Q8: I ordered a cork yoga mat, and there's a small chunk missing from the edge. Is this normal?
A: A small irregularity on the edge of a cork product can occur naturally, but a missing chunk indicates damage or a manufacturing defect. Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement mat will be provided. Minor cork texture variations are normal; missing material is not.

Q9: My natural loofah scrubber developed black spots after a few days in the shower. Is it mold?
A: Yes, black spots are likely mold/mildew. Natural loofahs need to dry completely between uses (hang in a well-ventilated area, not in a damp shower corner). Mold from improper drying is not a defect. However, if the spots appeared immediately after the first use despite proper drying, report within 7 days for evaluation. Always squeeze out excess water and hang to dry.

Q10: Can I return a refillable cleaning product if I mixed the concentrate incorrectly and it doesn't clean well?
A: No. Opened and mixed cleaning products are non-returnable. Dilution ratios are specified on the packaging. Improper mixing is user error. If the concentrate itself was defective (e.g., separated, spoiled, bad odor from a sealed pouch), that would be a defect.

Q11: I bought a "plantable" greeting card with seeds embedded. I planted it, but nothing grew. Can I get a refund?
A: Seed germination depends on planting conditions (soil, water, sunlight, depth, temperature). Plantable paper needs to be kept moist and warm. If you followed the provided planting instructions and nothing germinated after 3-4 weeks, it may indicate non-viable seeds. Report with details of your planting method. We will evaluate and may offer a replacement or refund. Seed viability is never 100%, but the entire card failing is unusual.

Q12: My stainless steel water bottle has a metallic taste that won't go away after washing. Is it defective?
A: A slight metallic taste in a new stainless steel bottle can occur if there are manufacturing residues. Wash thoroughly with warm soapy water, then soak with a baking soda and water solution overnight. Rinse well. If the metallic taste persists after thorough cleaning, report within 7 days. It may be a lower-grade steel issue.

Q13: I ordered a hemp bag, and the color is significantly different from the product image. It's much darker. Is this returnable?
A: Natural dyes and hemp fabric absorb dye differently than synthetic fabrics. Some color variation is normal. If the color is fundamentally different (e.g., ordered cream, received dark brown), photograph in natural light and report within 7 days. Significant color discrepancies are eligible for return.

Q14: My compostable phone case is warping after a few weeks of use. Is this normal?
A: Compostable and plant-based phone cases are designed to eventually break down in compost conditions. However, they should remain stable under normal use for their intended lifespan (typically 6-12 months). Premature warping after a few weeks indicates a material defect. Report within 7 days. A replacement will be provided.

Q15: Can I return an opened tin of natural deodorant if it irritated my skin?
A: Opened personal care products are non-returnable for hygiene and safety. Skin reactions to natural deodorants (often from baking soda or essential oils) are an individual sensitivity issue. Patch test on a small area of skin before full use. Discontinue if irritation occurs. We recommend researching ingredients before purchasing.

Q16: I bought "recycled plastic" outdoor furniture. It arrived with rough, sharp edges. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. Recycled plastic furniture should be finished smooth and safe for use. Sharp, rough edges indicate poor manufacturing quality control. Report within 7 days with photos. A replacement or refund will be provided. This is a finishing defect, not a characteristic of recycled material.

Q17: My organic cotton bedsheet shrank more than expected after washing according to care instructions. Is this a defect?
A: Organic cotton can shrink more than conventional cotton (3-5% is normal). If the shrinkage is excessive (beyond 8-10%) despite following cold wash and low-heat dry/line dry instructions, report within 7 days. Measure the sheet against the original listed dimensions. Excessive shrinkage is a manufacturing defect (improper pre-shrinking).

Q18: I bought a shampoo bar, and it's melting into a mushy blob in my shower. Is it supposed to do that?
A: Shampoo bars need to dry out between uses on a well-draining soap dish (not in a puddle of water). Mushiness indicates it's sitting in water. This is a usage/care issue, not a product defect. Use a slotted soap dish or wire rack. A properly cared-for shampoo bar should last 50-80 washes.

Q19: My bamboo furniture has a small crack developing at a joint. Can I return it?
A: A crack at a structural joint is a defect that compromises the furniture's integrity. Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement or refund will be provided. Bamboo can develop minor surface cracks (checking) from humidity changes, which are natural; structural joint cracks are not.

Q20: Can I return an eco-friendly laundry detergent sheet if it didn't dissolve fully in my washing machine?
A: Check the water temperature (some sheets require warm water to fully dissolve) and that the machine isn't overloaded (water needs to circulate). If the sheet doesn't dissolve in the recommended conditions (e.g., warm water, proper load size), report within 7 days with details. A formulation defect may exist.

Q21: I ordered a set of reusable produce bags. One bag has a hole in the mesh. Is this returnable?
A: Yes. A hole in a new mesh produce bag is a defect. Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement bag or set will be provided. Reusable produce bags should be intact to hold fruits and vegetables.

Q22: My coconut shell bowl has a crack and leaks when I put liquid in it. Is this normal for coconut products?
A: A coconut shell bowl should be fully sealed and waterproof. A leak indicates a crack that wasn't properly sealed. Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement bowl will be shipped. Coconut shell bowls should not leak — they're sealed with food-safe coatings.

Q23: I bought a natural rubber yoga mat. The smell is overwhelming and gives me a headache. Can I return it?
A: Natural rubber has a distinct smell that some people are sensitive to. This smell typically dissipates with airing out (3-7 days). Unroll the mat and air it in a well-ventilated area. If the smell is excessively chemical-like (not just rubber) or persists intolerably after a week, report within 7 days. A return or exchange for a different material mat (cork, TPE) may be possible.

Q24: Can I return a bamboo straw that split after a few uses?
A: Bamboo straws should last for many uses with proper care (hand washing, occasional oiling, thorough drying). Splitting after a few uses indicates a defect in the bamboo or curing process. Report within 7 days. A replacement straw or set will be provided.

Q25: My upcycled sari rug has a small stain. Is this part of the "upcycled character" or a defect?
A: Upcycled sari products are made from pre-loved saris. Minor imperfections, slight discoloration, and weave variations are part of their unique history. However, a prominent, fresh, or unappealing stain that wasn't visible in the listing photos may be a defect. Report within 7 days with a comparison photo. Each case is evaluated individually for upcycled products.

Q26: I bought a "BPA-free" plastic lunchbox. It smells strongly of plastic and the smell transfers to food. Is this normal?
A: BPA-free plastic can still have a mild initial plastic odor. Wash thoroughly and air out. If the smell persists and transfers to food, it may indicate a low-quality material or a misleading "BPA-free" claim (other bisphenols like BPS can also leach). Report within 7 days. We will investigate the material quality.

Q27: My solar-powered phone charger doesn't charge my phone at all, even in direct sunlight. Is it defective?
A: Check if the charger has an indicator light showing it's receiving solar power. Ensure the cable is working and the phone accepts charge from other sources. If the charger shows solar input but no output, or doesn't power on at all, report within 7 days. A replacement will be shipped. Solar charging speed is slow; this is about non-functionality, not speed.

Q28: Can I return an opened bag of biodegradable dog waste bags if they start breaking down in the roll before use?
A: Biodegradable bags should remain stable in storage. Premature degradation (bags tearing, becoming brittle) indicates the roll was exposed to moisture or heat, triggering the breakdown process. Report within 7 days with a photo. This is a storage/packaging defect. A replacement roll will be provided.

Q29: I ordered a fair-trade, hand-woven scarf. The weave is uneven in places. Is this a defect?
A: Uneven weave in a hand-woven scarf is a characteristic of the handloom process and is not a defect. It shows the human hand behind the product and distinguishes it from machine-made items. Celebrate the uniqueness. If the unevenness is extreme and compromises the scarf's structure (holes, large gaps), that would be a defect.

Q30: My reusable makeup remover pads are shedding lint after the first wash. Is this normal?
A: Some initial lint shedding can occur with new cotton/bamboo pads. However, excessive shedding that doesn't stop after 2-3 washes indicates poor fabric quality. Wash them inside a mesh laundry bag first. If shedding continues excessively, report within 7 days. A replacement set will be provided.
