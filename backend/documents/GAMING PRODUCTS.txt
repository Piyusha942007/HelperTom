SECTION 1: RETURNS & REPLACEMENT POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Gaming Consoles (PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo, Handhelds): 7 days (Replacement Only for defects).
- Gaming Controllers, Joysticks, and Steering Wheels: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects).
- Gaming Headsets and Audio Gear: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects; hygiene restrictions apply).
- Gaming Keyboards, Mice, and Mousepads: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects).
- Gaming Monitors: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects; follows Electronics policy).
- Gaming Chairs and Desks: 10 days from delivery.
- Physical Game Discs and Cartridges: 7 days (unopened/sealed only).
- Digital Game Codes, Gift Cards, and In-Game Currency: Non-returnable and non-refundable once revealed or accessed.
- Gaming Accessories (Skins, Cases, Charging Docks, Stands): 7 days.
- Collectibles, Action Figures, and Merchandise: 7 days.
- VR Headsets and Accessories: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects; hygiene restrictions apply).

1.2 Condition for Returns
- Gaming Consoles: Must be in original packaging with all accessories (controllers, cables, manuals). Factory reset must be performed, and all accounts (PlayStation Network, Xbox Live, Nintendo Account) must be removed. Consoles with activated software, downloaded games, or logged-in accounts will be rejected.
- Controllers: Must show no signs of use — no thumbstick wear, button shine, or grip marks. Disconnected controllers that have been paired are considered used.
- Headsets: Hygiene restrictions apply. In-ear gaming earbuds are non-returnable once seal is broken. Over-ear headsets can be returned if unused with all protective films on ear cushions.
- Physical Games: Must be in original sealed packaging. Opened games (shrink-wrap removed, case opened) are non-returnable. Digital code redemption is final.
- Gaming Chairs: Must be unassembled or in like-new condition. Chairs that have been sat in for extended periods show upholstery compression and are non-returnable for change of mind.
- VR Headsets: Hygiene restrictions apply. VR headsets that contact the face and have been worn are non-returnable unless defective. The facial interface foam must be clean and unused.

1.3 Defective Product Policy
- Defects covered:
  - Console: Dead on Arrival, no power, no display output, disc drive failure, overheating, excessive fan noise, system crashes
  - Controllers: Stick drift (unintended movement), unresponsive buttons, trigger issues, connection drops, battery failure
  - Headsets: No audio, distorted audio, microphone not working, static/white noise
  - Keyboards/Mice: Non-functional keys/clicks, double-clicking, ghosting, RGB lighting failure, connectivity issues
  - Gaming Monitors: Dead pixels (above manufacturer threshold), backlight bleeding, no display, flickering
  - Physical Games: Disc unreadable, disc cracked, wrong game inside sealed case
  - Gaming Chairs: Broken armrests, defective gas lift, torn upholstery out of box, broken casters
  - VR Headsets: Display defects, tracking failures, controller pairing issues, lens defects

- Defects NOT covered:
  - Stick drift developing after extensive use (normal wear on potentiometer joysticks)
  - Controller damage from drops, rage-quits, or aggressive use
  - Headset cable damage from pulling, bending, or pet chewing
  - Performance complaints (FPS not meeting expectations on a specific game)
  - Fan noise under heavy gaming load (normal; consoles ramp up fans when hot)
  - "Coil whine" from GPU/PSU (electrical noise, common and not a defect)
  - Cosmetic wear: shiny keys from use, thumbstick rubber wear, mouse feet wear
  - Download speed or Wi-Fi performance (depends on home network)
  - Game difficulty, story, or content dissatisfaction
  - Console banned from online services due to user behavior (violation of Terms of Service)

1.4 Digital Products Policy
- Digital game codes, DLC (Downloadable Content), subscription codes (PS Plus, Xbox Game Pass, Nintendo Online), and in-game currency (V-Bucks, FIFA Points, etc.) are delivered digitally.
- Once the code is displayed/revealed to the customer or the digital access is granted, the product is non-returnable and non-refundable.
- Accidental purchases of digital products are not refunded. Verify the platform, region, and product before completing purchase.
- Codes that are invalid, already redeemed, or region-locked incorrectly (despite the listing stating the correct region) are treated as defective and are eligible for replacement.

1.5 Replacement Process
- Step 1: Report the defect within 7 days with detailed description and photo/video evidence.
- Step 2: For consoles and complex issues, a remote troubleshooting session or video call may be scheduled.
- Step 3: Return pickup arranged. Product undergoes inspection (3-5 business days; consoles may take 5-7).
- Step 4: If confirmed defective, a replacement unit is shipped. If out of stock or discontinued, a refund is processed.

1.6 Refund Timelines
- Defect replacements (confirmed): Shipped in 5-10 business days.
- Refunds (when replacement unavailable): 7-10 business days after confirmation.

SECTION 5: GAMING FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: I bought a PS5, and it keeps crashing after 30 minutes of gameplay. The screen goes black and the console turns off. Is it defective?
A: This could indicate overheating (check ventilation — ensure the console has open space on all sides and isn't in a cabinet), or a hardware fault. Ensure the system software is updated. If crashing persists with proper ventilation, report within 7 days with a video showing the crash. A replacement will be provided.

Q2: My new gaming controller has stick drift straight out of the box. I haven't even played a game yet. What should I do?
A: Stick drift out of the box is a clear manufacturing defect. Calibrate the controller in your console/PC settings first to ensure it's not a calibration issue. If drift persists, report within 7 days with a video showing the unintended movement on the calibration screen. A replacement controller will be shipped.

Q3: I bought a physical game disc. The box was sealed, but when I opened it, the wrong game disc was inside. What do I do?
A: This is a rare packaging error at the manufacturer. Do not play the wrong game (scratches could be blamed on you). Photograph the wrong disc next to the game case. Report within 7 days. A replacement with the correct game will be shipped. This is a known manufacturing issue, and we handle it promptly.

Q4: Can I return a gaming headset if I don't like the surround sound effect quality?
A: Sound quality and virtual surround sound preferences are subjective and not defects. If the headset is functioning as per specifications (all drivers working, surround sound mode activates), it cannot be returned for sound preference. Additionally, over-ear headsets have hygiene restrictions if worn. Read professional reviews for sound signature before purchasing.

Q5: I redeemed a digital game code, but it's the wrong region and won't activate on my account. The listing said "India Region." What now?
A: Report immediately. A region mismatch is a seller/product error. Screenshot the error message showing the region lock and the listing showing "India Region." A replacement code for the correct region or a full refund will be provided. Do not attempt to create accounts in other regions to bypass this.

Q6: My gaming monitor has a single dead pixel right in the center of the screen. Is this covered for replacement?
A: Dead pixel policies vary by manufacturer. Most require a minimum of 3-5 dead or bright pixels, or pixels in a cluster, before accepting a replacement. A single dead pixel, while annoying, may not meet the manufacturer's threshold. Report within 7 days with a close-up photo. We will review against the specific monitor brand's pixel policy.

Q7: Can I return a gaming chair that I assembled and sat in for a week but find it hurts my back?
A: Assembled and used gaming chairs are non-returnable for comfort reasons. Ergonomic fit is highly individual. Research chair dimensions, lumbar support type, and user reviews before purchasing. Some high-end chair brands offer a "comfort guarantee" trial period — check the product page for this feature.

Q8: My Nintendo Switch won't read any game cartridges. Digital games work fine. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. A cartridge slot failure on a new console is a defect. Ensure the system is updated. Try multiple known working cartridges. If none work, report within 7 days. A replacement console will be provided. The cartridge slot is a critical component.

Q9: I bought a gaming keyboard, and several RGB lights are showing the wrong colors. Is this a defect?
A: First, check the keyboard software settings. Some keys may be set to different colors for game profiles. Reset the keyboard to factory defaults. If specific LEDs still show the wrong color (e.g., green instead of blue), the LEDs are defective. Report within 7 days with a photo/video. A replacement keyboard will be shipped.

Q10: Can I return a digital PlayStation Store gift card after scratching and revealing the code? I changed my mind.
A: No. Digital codes are non-returnable and non-refundable once the code is revealed or delivered. The code is considered activated. This is an industry-standard policy across all platforms (Steam, PSN, Xbox, Nintendo eShop) to prevent fraud.

Q11: My gaming mouse has double-clicking issues right out of the box. I click once, and it registers twice. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. Double-clicking on a new mouse is a switch defect (even though it's a common issue with older mice, it shouldn't be present out of the box). Report within 7 days with a video showing the double-click test (many websites can detect it). A replacement mouse will be shipped.

Q12: I bought a VR headset, and the lenses have a visible scratch/stain on the inside. Is this normal?
A: No. Scratches or stains on internal lenses are defects. Photograph them clearly. Report within 48 hours. A replacement VR headset will be shipped. Do not attempt to clean internal lenses with anything other than the provided microfiber cloth.

Q13: Can I return a collector's edition game if the statue inside is damaged but the game disc is sealed?
A: The collector's edition is a single product bundle. If any component (statue, artbook, steelbook) is damaged, the entire collector's edition is defective. Report within 7 days with photos of the damaged component. A replacement collector's edition will be shipped (subject to limited availability; a partial refund or full refund may be offered if out of stock).

Q14: My console's fan is extremely loud — it sounds like a jet engine even when idle on the home screen. Is this normal?
A: Consoles under load can get loud, but a console that is excessively loud even at idle or in menus may have a fan defect or poor thermal paste application. Record a video with the noise level in a quiet room. Report within 7 days. This will be compared against normal operating noise levels for that model.

Q15: I ordered a gaming headset stand with USB hub. The USB ports don't work, but the stand itself is fine. Can I get a partial refund or replacement?
A: Report within 7 days with a video showing the non-functional USB ports. Since the USB hub is an integral feature, the product is defective. A full replacement will be shipped. Partial refunds are possible if you prefer to keep the stand as just a stand.

Q16: Can I return a physical game if I found the story boring and finished it in 10 hours?
A: No. Physical games are non-returnable once the shrink-wrap seal is broken. Content satisfaction is not a return reason. Consider selling or trading in the used game instead. Our platform may have a trade-in program in the future.

Q17: My gaming controller's battery drains from 100% to 0% in under 2 hours. The specs say 10+ hours. Is it defective?
A: Yes. Severe battery drain indicating a battery defect. Ensure the controller is fully updated and vibration/lighting features are not draining it excessively (turn off vibration and dim lights as a test). If it still drains extremely fast, report within 7 days. A replacement controller will be provided.

Q18: I bought a skin/wrap for my console. I applied it, and it's full of bubbles and looks terrible. Can I return it?
A: No. Once applied, skins and wraps are non-returnable as the adhesive is used. Application quality depends on patience and technique. We recommend professional installation for complex console wraps. Watch application tutorials before attempting.

Q19: My gaming monitor has significant backlight bleeding along the edges, visible even during gameplay. Is this a defect?
A: Some backlight bleed (IPS glow) is common in LCD monitors. If the bleeding is excessive and visible during normal content (not just a black screen in a dark room), photograph it in a dimly lit room during gameplay. Report within 7 days. Our team evaluates against acceptable tolerance levels for the specific panel type.

Q20: I bought an Xbox Game Pass Ultimate code, but it says it's already been redeemed. What do I do?
A: Report immediately. Screenshot the redemption error page showing the code and the "already redeemed" message. We will investigate. If the code was compromised before delivery, a replacement code will be issued. Code theft is rare but taken seriously. Do not share the code with anyone.

Q21: Can I return an arcade stick (fight stick) if I don't like the button sensitivity?
A: Arcade stick buttons and joysticks have specific switch types (e.g., Sanwa, Hayabusa) with known tactile characteristics. If the stick is functioning correctly, button feel preference is not a return reason. Research the components before buying. If a button is mechanically defective (sticking, not registering), that's covered.

Q22: My new console has a cosmetic scratch on the glossy plastic part right out of the box. Can I exchange it?
A: Yes. A cosmetic defect on a brand-new, sealed console is not acceptable. Photograph the scratch clearly with the console's serial number visible. Report within 48 hours. A replacement console will be provided. Unboxing video is crucial for cosmetic defect claims.

Q23: I bought a racing wheel and pedal set. The force feedback feels weak compared to my friend's same model. Is it defective?
A: Force feedback strength depends on in-game settings, wheel driver settings, and the specific game. Ensure firmware is updated and settings match typical configurations. If the force feedback is significantly weaker than expected even at maximum settings, report within 7 days. A comparison video may help. The motor or belt may be defective.

Q24: Can I return a gaming desk if the carbon fiber wrap on the surface starts peeling after assembly?
A: Peeling surface material on a new desk is a manufacturing defect. Report within 10 days with photos. A replacement desk top or full desk replacement will be provided. Do not glue it down, as this voids the return.

Q25: My handheld gaming device (Steam Deck, ROG Ally) has a stuck pixel that's always bright white. Is this covered?
A: A bright (stuck) pixel is more noticeable than a dead (black) pixel. Report within 7 days with a close-up photo. Handheld device pixel policies vary. Bright pixels in the central viewing area are often covered. We will work with the manufacturer's policy to get a resolution.

Q26: I bought a gaming mousepad with RGB lighting. The lights work, but the surface has a permanent crease from being folded in the box. Is this returnable?
A: Yes. A permanent crease on a mousepad surface affects mouse tracking and is a packaging defect. Photograph the crease. Report within 7 days. Lay the mousepad flat with weight on it for 24 hours first. If the crease persists, a replacement will be shipped.

Q27: Can I return a pair of gaming glasses (blue light blocking) if they give me a headache?
A: If the glasses are unworn with tags and original packaging, yes, within 7 days. Once worn, they are non-returnable for hygiene (face contact). Headaches from new glasses can occur during an adjustment period, which doesn't indicate a defect.

Q28: My console's disc drive makes a loud grinding noise when inserting a disc, and the disc comes out with scratches. What should I do?
A: Stop using the disc drive immediately. A grinding disc drive that scratches discs is a serious mechanical defect. Report within 7 days with a video showing the noise and photos of scratched discs. A replacement console will be provided. Damaged discs caused by the defective drive will be replaced as well (within the return window).

Q29: I ordered a limited edition controller. The design on it is slightly misaligned/off-center compared to the product photos. Is this a defect?
A: For limited edition and custom-printed controllers, slight design variations are normal. However, significant misalignment (graphics clearly off-center, overlapping wrong areas) is a print defect. Report within 7 days with a side-by-side comparison photo. We will evaluate and replace if confirmed.

Q30: My gaming PC (pre-built) came with a GPU that's a different brand than what was listed. Specs are the same (e.g., RTX 4070). Can I return it?
A: If the listing specified a particular brand/model of GPU (e.g., "ASUS TUF RTX 4070") and you received a different brand (e.g., "MSI RTX 4070"), this is a product variation. If the listing says "RTX 4070" without specific brand, any brand is acceptable. If a specific brand was promised, report within 7 days. We will arrange a swap or partial refund for the price difference.
