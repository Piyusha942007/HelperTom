SECTION 1: RETURNS & REPLACEMENT POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Small Kitchen Appliances (Toasters, Kettles, Blenders, Mixer Grinders, Coffee Makers): 7 days from delivery (Replacement Only for defects).
- Large Kitchen Appliances (Refrigerators, Washing Machines, Dishwashers, Microwaves, Ovens): 10 days from delivery (Replacement Only for defects).
- Cookware and Kitchen Tools (Pans, Pots, Utensils, Knives): 7 days.
- Kitchen Storage and Containers: 7 days.
- Bakeware and Baking Tools: 7 days.
- Tableware, Dinnerware, and Glassware: 7 days (inspect upon delivery for breakage).
- Water Purifiers and RO Systems: 10 days (Replacement Only; installation and functionality inspection).
- Chimneys, Hobs, and Built-in Appliances: 10 days (Replacement Only).
- Disposables and Single-Use Kitchen Items: Non-returnable once packaging opened.

1.2 Condition for Returns
- Kitchen Appliances (Small): Must be unused, in original packaging with all accessories, manuals, and warranty cards. Appliances that have been plugged in and used (even once) are non-returnable for change of mind.
- Kitchen Appliances (Large): Delivered with Open-Box inspection. Any damage must be noted on the POD. Installed or used large appliances are non-returnable except for defects.
- Cookware and Utensils: Must be unused, unwashed, in original packaging. Signs of cooking (oil marks, food residue, scratches from use, heat discoloration) lead to rejection.
- Non-stick cookware: Must be completely untouched. Any scratch on the non-stick coating from stacking or handling is considered damage.
- Knives and Cutting Tools: Must be in sealed packaging. Opened knives are non-returnable for safety reasons.
- Glassware and Ceramics: Must be inspected upon delivery. Breakage claims after delivery are difficult to process. Photograph any damage immediately.
- Water Bottles and Thermoses: Must be unused, unwashed. Once used for beverages, non-returnable (hygiene).

1.3 Defective Product Policy
- Defects covered for Appliances:
  - Dead on Arrival: Appliance does not power on or function at all
  - Manufacturing defect: Malfunctioning motor, heating element failure, electrical short
  - Physical damage upon delivery: Dents, cracks, broken parts, shattered glass
  - Missing parts or accessories preventing normal operation
  - Wrong model, color, or capacity delivered
  - Excessive noise, vibration, or sparking during first use (safety hazard)
  - Non-stick coating peeling or blistering on first use (before any cooking)
  - Gas leak from gas appliances (hobs, stoves) upon connection by authorized technician
  - Water leak from water purifiers, dishwashers, or washing machines upon installation
  - Refrigerator not cooling, freezer not freezing, or excessive frost formation
  - Microwave not heating, turntable not rotating, sparking inside

- Defects NOT covered:
  - Normal operational sounds (compressor hum, motor whir, water flow sounds)
  - Minor cosmetic imperfections that don't affect function
  - Appliance not meeting personal performance expectations (e.g., blender not making smoothies as smooth as desired)
  - Capacity perception (a 7kg washing machine looking smaller than expected)
  - Rust or damage from improper cleaning or exposure to moisture
  - Non-stick coating damage from metal utensils, abrasive cleaning, or overheating
  - Glass breakage from thermal shock (pouring cold water into a hot glass dish)
  - Damage from power surges (use a voltage stabilizer/surge protector as recommended)
  - Clogged filters, pipes, or drains from user neglect
  - Pest/insect infestation in appliances after delivery
  - Consumable parts: filters, bulbs, gaskets (covered under warranty per brand terms)

1.4 Large Appliance Installation and Demo
- HelperTom provides free basic installation for large appliances (washing machines, refrigerators, dishwashers, chimneys, hobs, water purifiers, microwaves — built-in).
- Installation is performed by brand-authorized or HelperTom-trained technicians.
- The customer (or an adult representative) must be present during installation.
- During installation, the technician will:
  - Unpack and inspect for physical damage
  - Perform a basic functionality test (power on, water connection, gas connection)
  - Demonstrate basic operations
- Any defect identified during installation is documented by the technician, and a replacement or repair is initiated immediately.
- If the customer declines installation and self-installs, any damage during self-installation is not covered.

1.5 Replacement Process for Appliances
- Step 1: Report the defect within the return window (7 or 10 days) with photos/video.
- Step 2: A technical support call or technician visit is arranged to diagnose the issue.
- Step 3: If confirmed as a manufacturing defect:
  - Small appliances: Replacement unit shipped after pickup and inspection (5-7 business days)
  - Large appliances: Replacement unit delivered and installed within 7-10 business days
- Step 4: If the issue is due to installation error or user misuse, a service charge may apply.

1.6 Refund Timelines
- Refunds for appliances are only issued when replacement is not possible (model discontinued, out of stock for 15+ days).
- Refund to source: 7-10 business days after replacement cancellation confirmation.

SECTION 5: KITCHEN APPLIANCES FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: I bought a mixer grinder. The motor smells like it's burning the first time I used it. Is this normal?
A: A slight "new appliance" smell during the first use is normal as manufacturing residues burn off. However, a strong, acrid burning smell with smoke is a serious electrical defect. Stop using immediately. Report within 7 days with a description. A technician will inspect, and a replacement will be provided if confirmed defective.

Q2: My new non-stick pan's coating started peeling after the first use. I only used a silicone spatula. What should I do?
A: Peeling non-stick coating after first use with proper utensils is a clear manufacturing defect. Stop using the pan (ingested coating is a health concern). Photograph the peeling area and report within 7 days. A replacement pan will be provided.

Q3: I ordered a stainless steel refrigerator, but I received a black one. Can I exchange it?
A: Report immediately during Open-Box delivery. Do not sign the POD without noting the color discrepancy. If discovered after the delivery team leaves, report within 24 hours with a photo of the black fridge against your order showing "Stainless Steel." A replacement with the correct color will be arranged. Color is a fundamental product attribute.

Q4: My new washing machine shakes violently and "walks" across the floor during the spin cycle. Is it defective?
A: Excessive vibration is often due to:
- Transit bolts not being removed (critical — check the manual)
- Uneven flooring
- Unbalanced load
First, ensure the transit bolts have been removed by the installation technician. If removed and the machine still shakes violently on a level floor with a balanced load, report within 10 days. A technician will inspect for drum misalignment or suspension defects.

Q5: Can I return a toaster that toasts unevenly — one side is burnt, and the other is barely brown?
A: Uneven toasting can be a heating element defect. Test with different bread types and slots. If consistently uneven, report within 7 days with photos of the uneven toast. A replacement toaster will be shipped. Note that some premium toasters have a "bagel" setting that toasts one side more — ensure it's not a setting issue.

Q6: I bought a set of kitchen knives. One knife has a bent tip right out of the block. Is this returnable?
A: Yes. A bent tip is a manufacturing defect or transit damage. Photograph the bent knife. Report within 7 days. The knife set will be replaced. Do not attempt to bend the tip back, as this can cause the metal to snap.

Q7: My RO water purifier is leaking from under the unit after installation. The technician left an hour ago. What do I do?
A: Turn off the water supply to the purifier immediately. Contact support within 24 hours. A follow-up technician visit will be scheduled urgently. Leaks can damage your kitchen cabinetry. This is an installation defect, and a reinstallation or unit replacement will be done at no cost.

Q8: Can I return a pressure cooker if the gasket (rubber ring) seems loose and steam escapes from the side?
A: First, ensure the gasket is properly seated and the lid is locked correctly. A new gasket may need a couple of uses to seat perfectly. If steam still escapes dangerously after proper fitting, report within 7 days. A replacement gasket or cooker will be provided. Do not force-close or use a leaking pressure cooker.

Q9: I ordered an air fryer, and the digital display is flickering and unreadable. Is this a defect?
A: Yes, a malfunctioning display is a defect. Report within 7 days with a video showing the flickering. A replacement air fryer will be shipped. Display issues are covered under the Electronics-adjacent policy.

Q10: My new microwave makes a loud humming noise and doesn't heat food. The light and turntable work. What's wrong?
A: If the microwave powers on (light, turntable) but doesn't heat, the magnetron or high-voltage components may be defective. This is a manufacturing defect. Report within 7-10 days. A technician will inspect, and the unit will be replaced if confirmed.

Q11: Can I return a water bottle that I used once and now has a persistent smell of the drink I put in it?
A: No. Used water bottles are non-returnable for hygiene reasons. Odor retention depends on the bottle material (stainless steel, plastic, glass) and what was stored. Try cleaning with baking soda and vinegar. Choose glass or stainless steel for odor-prone beverages.

Q12: I bought a granite-coated kadai. The coating has a rough patch that food sticks to. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. A rough patch on a new non-stick/coated surface is a manufacturing defect (poor coating application). Report within 7 days with a photo showing the rough area. A replacement kadai will be provided. Do not use the kadai, as the rough patch will worsen with cooking.

Q13: My chimney's suction is very poor. It's not pulling smoke from my kitchen. The installation was done yesterday. What do I do?
A: First, check the ducting. If the duct pipe is too long, has too many bends, or the external vent is blocked, suction is reduced. Ensure the filters are clean (new chimneys shouldn't have clogged filters). If ducting is correct and suction is still poor, report within 10 days. A technician will inspect the motor and fan. A replacement may be necessary.

Q14: Can I return a set of ceramic dinner plates if the color is slightly different from the product images?
A: Minor color variations are expected due to screen calibration and glaze batch variations. If the color is fundamentally different (e.g., ordered white, received cream with a yellow tint), it may be a wrong product or listing error. Report with photos in natural light. Dinnerware sets are returnable within 7 days if unused and in original packaging.

Q15: I received a blender with a cracked jar. The motor base is fine. Can I just get a replacement jar?
A: Yes. Report within 48 hours with a photo of the cracked jar. We will ship a replacement jar. You don't need to return the entire blender. This is faster and more convenient for you.

Q16: My new refrigerator makes a loud gurgling/bubbling sound. It's cooling fine, but the noise is annoying. Is this normal?
A: Gurgling or bubbling sounds are normal — they're the refrigerant flowing through the coils. Different refrigerators have different sound profiles. This is not a defect. If the noise is a loud banging, clicking, or grinding, that would be abnormal and should be reported.

Q17: I bought a cast iron skillet, and it arrived with a small patch of rust. Can I return it?
A: Surface rust on a new cast iron skillet indicates inadequate factory seasoning or moisture exposure during storage. Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement or refund will be provided. Light surface rust can also be removed by scrubbing and re-seasoning, but a new product should arrive rust-free.

Q18: Can I return a coffee maker that brews coffee that's not hot enough for my liking?
A: Brew temperature is a specification. Check the product's brew temperature against your preference. If the coffee maker is brewing at the advertised temperature (typically 85-95°C), it's functioning correctly. "Not hot enough" is a preference, not a defect. If it's brewing significantly below the advertised temperature, report within 7 days for evaluation.

Q19: My dishwasher isn't cleaning dishes properly. Dishes come out with food residue still on them. Is it defective?
A: First, ensure you're loading dishes correctly (not blocking spray arms), using the right detergent, and not overloading. Check that the spray arms spin freely and the filters are clean. If the issue persists across multiple cycles with correct loading, report within 10 days. A technician will inspect for pump or spray arm defects.

Q20: I bought a kitchen chimney, and the touch control panel is unresponsive. I have to press multiple times. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. An unresponsive touch panel is an electronic defect. Report within 10 days with a video showing the unresponsiveness. A technician will inspect and replace the control panel or the entire unit if necessary.

Q21: Can I return a wooden chopping board that arrived with a crack along the grain?
A: Yes. A crack is a defect that compromises the board's structure and creates a hygiene issue (food/bacteria trapped in cracks). Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement board will be shipped.

Q22: My induction cooktop shows an error code and won't work with my induction-compatible pan. What should I do?
A: Check the error code in the manual. Common causes: incompatible pan (test with a magnet — it must stick), pan too small, or pan not centered. If the error persists with a known compatible pan, report within 7 days. A replacement cooktop will be shipped.

Q23: I ordered a 4-burner gas hob, but the installation technician says it requires a different gas connection than what I have at home. Can I return it?
A: Gas hobs are built-in appliances. If the gas type (LPG vs. PNG) or connection doesn't match your home setup, and this wasn't checked before ordering, the hob is not defective. However, within the 10-day window, we may accommodate a return on a case-by-case basis, with a restocking fee. It's crucial to check gas compatibility before ordering built-in appliances.

Q24: My new OTG oven's temperature dial is inaccurate — it burns cookies at the "bake" setting. Is this a defect?
A: Oven thermostats can have slight inaccuracies. Use an oven thermometer to verify the actual temperature. If the temperature deviates by more than 25°F (15°C) from the dial setting, it's a defect. Report within 7 days with thermometer readings. A technician will recalibrate or replace the oven.

Q25: Can I return a set of measuring cups if the measurement markings are already fading after one wash?
A: Yes. Fading markings after minimal use is a manufacturing defect (poor printing/etching). Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement set will be provided. Quality measuring cups should have etched or durable printed markings.

Q26: My food processor's safety interlock mechanism is faulty — it runs even when the bowl isn't locked. This seems dangerous.
A: This is a SERIOUS safety defect. Stop using the appliance immediately. A faulty safety interlock can cause severe injury. Report within 7 days (immediately). A replacement will be shipped with priority. Do not attempt to use or bypass the safety mechanism. The batch may be investigated.

Q27: I received a glass baking dish with a small chip on the rim. Can I still use it, or should I return it?
A: Do not use a chipped glass baking dish. Chips create stress points that can cause the dish to shatter in the oven (thermal stress). Report within 48 hours with a photo of the chip. A replacement dish will be shipped. Safety first.

Q28: Can I return a water purifier if the TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) of the output water is not as low as I expected?
A: RO water purifiers reduce TDS by a certain percentage (typically 90-95%). If your input TDS is high (e.g., 1000 ppm), the output will be around 50-100 ppm, which is normal. Check the reduction rate in the specifications. If the purifier is not achieving the rated reduction, report within 10 days for inspection. TDS preference is subjective; some prefer slightly higher TDS for taste.

Q29: My new juicer's pulp is very wet, and the juice yield is low. Is this a defect or normal?
A: Juice yield depends on the produce (freshness, type, water content). Some juicers (centrifugal) naturally produce wetter pulp than others (masticating/slow juicers). Check the juicer type and typical yield expectations. If the yield is significantly lower than reviews and specifications suggest for the same produce, report within 7 days. A comparison with a same-model demonstration may be needed.

Q30: I bought a stainless steel cookware set. The pots have rainbow-like discoloration inside after the first wash. Is this normal?
A: Rainbow discoloration (heat tint) on stainless steel is caused by mineral deposits from water drying on the surface. It's cosmetic and harmless. Clean with vinegar or a stainless steel cleaner. It is not a defect. However, if the discoloration doesn't clean off and is accompanied by pitting or rust spots, that could indicate poor-quality steel. Report within 7 days if cleaning doesn't resolve it.
