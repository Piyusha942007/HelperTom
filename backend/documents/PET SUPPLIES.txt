SECTION 1: RETURNS & REFUND POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Pet Food and Treats: 7 days from the date of delivery (unopened packs only).
- Pet Health and Grooming Products: 7 days from delivery.
- Pet Toys and Accessories: 7 days from delivery.
- Pet Beds, Crates, and Carriers: 7 days from delivery.
- Pet Clothing and Collars: 7 days from delivery.
- Aquariums, Terrariums, and Cages: 10 days from delivery.
- Live Fish, Plants, and Live Animals: Non-returnable. Dead-on-Arrival claims handled separately.
- Prescription Pet Medications: Non-returnable under any circumstances as per Indian pharmacy regulations.

1.2 Condition for Returns
- Pet Food: Must be unopened, with the original factory seal intact. Opened bags or cans of pet food are non-returnable (even if the pet refuses to eat it).
- Treats and Chews: Unopened packaging only. Opened packs are non-returnable.
- Pet Health Products (Shampoos, Conditioners, Flea Treatments, Supplements): Unopened with safety seal intact.
- Pet Toys: Must be unused. Teeth marks, slobber, dirt, or any sign of play makes them non-returnable.
- Pet Beds and Blankets: Must be clean, without pet hair, odors, or stains. Bedding that has been lain on by a pet is non-returnable.
- Crates and Carriers: Must be unassembled or in like-new condition with no signs of pet presence (hair, scratches, chew marks).
- Pet Clothing and Collars: Unworn with tags attached. Pet hair, odor, or stretching makes them non-returnable.
- Leashes and Harnesses: Unused. Any sign of wear, stretching, or chewing leads to rejection.
- Litter Boxes and Litter: Unused. Litter boxes with any litter residue are non-returnable.
- Aquariums: Must be unused, unfilled, and in original packaging with all seals. Once water has been added, non-returnable.
- Grooming Tools (Brushes, Clippers, Nail Trimmers): Unused. Pet hair in brushes makes them non-returnable (hygiene).

1.3 Defective Product Policy
- Defects covered:
  - Toys that break or splinter on first supervised use (choking/ingestion hazard)
  - Beds with torn seams, loose stuffing, or broken zippers out of the box
  - Crates with bent bars, broken latches, or sharp edges
  - Collars and harnesses with faulty buckles or D-rings that break under normal pressure
  - Leashes with frayed stitching or broken clips
  - Grooming clippers that don't turn on or malfunction
  - Aquariums with cracked glass or leaking seals when filled for the first time
  - Automatic feeders or water fountains that don't function
  - Heating pads or mats that don't heat up
  - Damaged pet food packaging: punctured bags, dented cans, broken seals
  - Expired pet food or treats at the time of delivery
  - Infested pet food (insects, weevils in sealed bags)
  - Wrong flavor, formula, or size delivered

- Defects NOT covered:
  - Toys destroyed by aggressive chewers (choose toys rated for your pet's chew strength)
  - Beds chewed or scratched by the pet
  - Fading or wear of fabric items from use and washing
  - Pet refusing to eat a particular food or flavor
  - Digestive upset from food transitions (always transition gradually over 7-10 days)
  - Collars or harnesses chewed through by the pet
  - Aquarium glass broken from impact after delivery
  - Normal wear on grooming tools

1.4 Pet Food and Treats Specifics
- Pet food is a sensitive consumable. Returns are strictly limited to unopened products with intact seals.
- Transitioning to a new food should be done gradually (mixing old and new food over 7-10 days). Sudden changes causing digestive issues are not a product defect.
- If a pet shows signs of an allergic reaction (skin rashes, vomiting, diarrhea) potentially linked to a new food, discontinue use and consult a veterinarian. Document the issue. HelperTom may request veterinary documentation for investigation purposes but does not guarantee a refund for opened food.
- Prescription or veterinary diet foods: Non-returnable once delivered, even if unopened, as per health regulations.

1.5 Live Animals and Aquatic Life
- HelperTom does not sell live animals, birds, or reptiles.
- Live aquarium fish and aquatic plants (where available through verified sellers): Dead-on-Arrival (DOA) guarantee applies.
- DOA claims must be reported within 6 hours of delivery with a clear photo/video of the dead fish/plant in the unopened transport bag.
- DOA refund or reshipment is at the seller's discretion per their specific guarantee policy. This is clearly stated on the product page.
- Once the bag is opened and fish/plants are introduced to the aquarium, no claims are accepted.
- Acclimatization losses (fish dying after introduction to the tank) are not covered, as water parameter differences are the owner's responsibility.

1.6 Refund Timelines
- Pet Supplies: 5-7 business days after quality check for returns.
- Defective/Damaged items with photo evidence: Refund within 3-5 business days.
- DOA claims for live fish/plants: Refund or reshipment per seller policy, typically within 3-5 business days.

SECTION 4: PET SUPPLIES FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: I bought a 15kg bag of dog food, but my dog refuses to eat it. Can I return the opened bag?
A: No. Opened pet food bags are non-returnable, even if your pet refuses to eat the food. We recommend buying the smallest available pack first to test palatability before committing to a large bag. Some brands offer palatability guarantees directly; check the brand's website.

Q2: My puppy destroyed a "durable" chew toy in 10 minutes. The packaging said it was for aggressive chewers. Can I return it?
A: If the toy was specifically marketed for aggressive/power chewers and was destroyed under normal supervised chewing within minutes, it may be a defect or false advertising. Report within 7 days with photos of the destroyed toy and the packaging showing the "aggressive chewer" claim. We will investigate and may offer a refund or replacement.

Q3: I received cat litter with a torn bag, and there's litter dust everywhere. What should I do?
A: Photograph the torn bag and the spillage. Report within 48 hours. We will ship a replacement bag. You don't need to return the spilled litter; dispose of it or use what's salvageable. We may ask for an image of the outer shipping box to check for transit damage.

Q4: Can I return a dog bed that my dog slept in once and now it's covered in fur?
A: No. A pet bed with pet hair, dander, odor, or any sign of use is non-returnable for hygiene reasons. We recommend placing a washable cover or blanket over the bed for the first few nights if you are unsure about keeping it. Size and material should be evaluated before allowing your pet to use it.

Q5: The automatic cat feeder I bought doesn't dispense food. I've tried new batteries and resetting it. Is it defective?
A: First, ensure you're using the correct size of kibble (some feeders jam with large kibble) and the hopper isn't overfilled. If the issue persists, report within 7 days with a video showing the malfunction. A replacement feeder will be shipped. This follows the Electronics policy.

Q6: I ordered a dog harness, and the buckle broke the first time I put it on my dog. He wasn't even pulling. What now?
A: A buckle breaking under normal fitting conditions is a defect and a safety hazard (dog could escape). Report within 7 days with a photo of the broken buckle. Stop using the harness. A replacement or refund will be provided.

Q7: My cat scratching post is wobbling and falling over when my cat uses it. Is this a defect?
A: If the scratching post is assembled correctly per instructions and still wobbles or tips over under a cat's normal weight/use, it is a stability defect. Report within 7 days with a video. A replacement or refund will be provided.

Q8: I bought an aquarium online. When I filled it with water, I noticed a slow leak from the bottom seal. What do I do?
A: Immediately drain the aquarium to prevent water damage to your property. Photograph the leak area (soapy water can help show bubbles at the leak point). Report within the 10-day window. This is a manufacturing defect. A replacement aquarium will be shipped.

Q9: Can I return dog nail clippers that I used once but didn't like the grip?
A: No. Used grooming tools with pet contact are non-returnable for hygiene reasons. The grip design is visible in product images and should be evaluated before purchase and use.

Q10: I received a bag of dog treats that expires next month. The product page didn't mention short expiry. Is this acceptable?
A: No. Unless explicitly marked as "Short Expiry Clearance," pet treats should have a minimum of 3 months shelf life. Report within 7 days with a photo of the expiry date. A replacement or refund will be provided.

Q11: My bird cage arrived with bent bars. The outer box looks okay, but the cage is damaged. What should I do?
A: This suggests the damage occurred before packaging. Photograph the bent bars clearly and report within 48 hours. A replacement cage will be shipped. Do not attempt to bend the bars back, as this could weaken the metal and create sharp edges.

Q12: Can I return an opened bottle of dog shampoo if the fragrance is too strong?
A: No. Opened pet grooming products are non-returnable for fragrance preferences. The scent is subjective. Buy smaller bottles to test if your pet or you are sensitive to fragrances.

Q13: I ordered a pet carrier for air travel, but it's 2 inches too big per airline regulations. Can I return it?
A: Yes, if the carrier is unused, in original packaging, and within 7 days. You are responsible for checking airline-specific dimensions before ordering. We provide dimensions on the product page; airline compatibility is not guaranteed unless explicitly stated.

Q14: My dog's new leash has stitching coming undone near the clip after two walks. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. Stitching failing after minimal use is a manufacturing defect and a safety concern (leash could snap). Stop using the leash. Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement will be sent.

Q15: I bought live aquarium plants. They arrived brown and mushy. Are they dead?
A: Some aquarium plants shed leaves ("melting") during transit and acclimation — this can be normal, and they regrow. However, if the plants are entirely brown, mushy, and smell rotten, they are likely dead. Report within 6 hours with clear photos. Refer to the seller's DOA policy on the product page. A refund or reshipment may be issued.

Q16: Can I return a dog coat that is too big for my dog, even though I measured before ordering?
A: Yes, if the coat is unworn, with tags attached, and free of pet hair or odor. Returns for size issues are allowed within 7 days if the item is in new condition. Always measure your pet carefully (chest girth, neck, back length) before ordering.

Q17: My cat fountain water pump stopped working after 3 days. I cleaned the filter as instructed. Is it defective?
A: First, check for mineral buildup or debris in the pump impeller (even new pumps can clog if water is hard). Disassemble and clean thoroughly. If it still doesn't work, report within 7 days with a video. A replacement pump or fountain will be sent.

Q18: I ordered a reptile heating mat, but it doesn't get warm even after being plugged in for hours. What should I do?
A: Heating mats warm up gradually and may feel only slightly warm to the human touch (they're designed to raise the enclosure floor temperature, not feel hot). Use a thermometer to check the surface temperature. If it's not reaching the rated temperature, report within 7 days. A replacement will be sent. This is critical for reptile health.

Q19: Can I return an opened box of dog dental chews because they're too hard for my senior dog?
A: No. Opened consumable treats are non-returnable. Check the product description for chew hardness and size suitability. Many brands offer senior-specific softer chews.

Q20: My rabbit's water bottle leaks constantly from the nozzle. I've checked the seal and ball bearing. Is it defective?
A: Yes. A continuously leaking water bottle is a defect (it can soak the bedding and leave the pet without water). Report within 7 days with a video showing the leak. A replacement will be sent.

Q21: I received a dog crate with a missing tray and handle. Can I get just those parts?
A: Yes. Report the missing parts within 7 days with photos of what you received. We will ship the missing tray and handle. You don't need to return the entire crate. This is much faster than a full return.

Q22: Can I return a pet stroller that I assembled but my dog refuses to get into?
A: Assembled pet strollers are non-returnable unless defective. A pet's refusal to use the stroller is not a product defect. We recommend introducing your pet to the stroller gradually (treats inside, short stationary sessions) before attempting walks.

Q23: My fish food container has a broken seal, and the pellets smell rancid. What do I do?
A: Do not feed it to your fish. Rancid smell indicates oxidation and spoilage. Report within 7 days with a photo of the broken seal. A replacement or refund will be provided. Spoiled fish food can harm aquarium water quality and fish health.

Q24: I bought a dog collar with a personalized nameplate. The name is spelled wrong. Can I return it?
A: If the spelling error was on HelperTom/seller's side (different from what you entered), it's a manufacturing defect for personalized items. Report within 7 days. A corrected collar will be made and shipped. If the error was in your order input, personalized items are non-returnable.

Q25: My cat's new toy has a bell inside that came loose within a day and is a choking hazard. Is this covered?
A: Yes. A toy breaking and creating a choking hazard under normal play is a serious safety defect. Report immediately within 7 days. Remove the toy from your cat. A refund will be processed, and the product may be flagged for safety review.

Q26: Can I return hamster bedding that I opened but found to be too dusty?
A: Opened bedding is generally non-returnable. However, if the dust level is excessive to the point of being harmful (visible dust cloud when handling), report within 7 days with a photo/video showing the dust. This would be a quality defect and will be reviewed case by case. Your hamster's respiratory health is important.

Q27: I ordered a large aquarium, and it arrived with a small chip on the corner of the glass. Is it safe to use?
A: Do NOT fill a chipped aquarium. Even a small chip can compromise structural integrity, leading to catastrophic failure when filled with water. Photograph the chip and report within 48 hours. A replacement aquarium will be shipped. Do not attempt to use or repair it.

Q28: My dog's new tennis ball split in half during the first game of fetch. Is this normal?
A: A tennis ball splitting on first use is a defect (unless your dog is an extremely powerful chewer and the ball wasn't rated for that). Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement or refund will be provided. For power chewers, look for balls specifically marketed as "indestructible" or "heavy-duty."

Q29: I bought freeze-dried raw dog food. The bag feels like it has moisture inside, and there are clumps. Is it spoiled?
A: Freeze-dried food should be completely dry and crisp. Moisture and clumping indicate the packaging seal was compromised, leading to rehydration and potential mold/bacterial growth. Do not feed it. Report within 7 days with photos showing the clumps and any condensation. A full refund or replacement will be provided.

Q30: Can I return a fish tank filter if it's too loud for my bedroom?
A: Aquarium filters produce some noise. If the noise level is significantly louder than the decibel rating specified (if listed) or is a grinding/rattling sound indicating a mechanical defect, report within 7 days with a video recording the noise. A replacement will be provided if confirmed defective. Subjective noise tolerance is not covered.
