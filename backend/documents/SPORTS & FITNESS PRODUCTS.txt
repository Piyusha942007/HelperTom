SECTION 1: RETURNS & REPLACEMENT POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Sports Equipment (Bats, Rackets, Balls, Protective Gear): 7 days from the date of delivery.
- Fitness Equipment (Treadmills, Exercise Bikes, Weights, Benches): 10 days from the date of delivery.
- Wearable Fitness Trackers and Smartwatches: 7 days (Replacement Only, follows Electronics policy).
- Apparel and Footwear (Sportswear, Jerseys, Sports Shoes): 7 days (follows Fashion & Clothing / Footwear policies).
- Consumables (Nutrition Supplements, Protein Powders, Energy Gels): Non-returnable once seal is broken.
- Yoga and Wellness Products (Mats, Blocks, Straps): 7 days.

1.2 Condition for Returns
- Sports equipment must be unused or used only for initial inspection and fit testing.
- Bats (Cricket, Baseball, Hockey): Must not show ball marks, scratches from play, or grip wear. Protective plastic wrap on grips must be intact.
- Rackets (Tennis, Badminton, Squash): Must not show string wear, frame scratches, or grip dirt. The original grip wrap must be intact.
- Balls (Cricket, Football, Basketball, Volleyball): Must not be inflated or used. Scuff marks, dirt, or pressure loss from use makes them non-returnable.
- Fitness Equipment: Must be in original packaging, unassembled (unless delivered pre-assembled with open-box inspection), with all parts, hardware, and manuals.
- Weights and Dumbbells: Chipped paint, rust, or signs of dropping are not covered after acceptance.
- Protective Gear (Helmets, Guards, Gloves): Must be unworn with tags. Helmets are non-returnable if the interior foam has been compressed by wearing.
- Yoga Mats: Must be unrolled but unused. Sweat marks, dirt, or creases from extended use lead to rejection.
- Mouthguards: Non-returnable for hygiene once packaging is opened.
- Swimwear and Swim Goggles: Non-returnable if hygiene seal is broken.

1.3 Defective Product Policy
- Defects covered:
  - Manufacturing defects: Cracked frames, loose joints, broken welds, delamination
  - Cricket bats with cracks (not surface cracks from knocking-in), broken handles
  - Rackets with broken strings upon arrival, bent frames
  - Fitness equipment with missing parts, non-functional electronics, motor issues (treadmills)
  - Exercise bikes with faulty resistance mechanisms, broken pedals
  - Dumbbells with loose plates, cracked rubber coating
  - Yoga mats with strong chemical odor beyond normal new-product smell
  - Inflatable balls that don't hold air (pin-hole leaks not caused by punctures)
  - Skipping ropes with frayed cables or broken handles

- Defects NOT covered:
  - Normal wear and tear from use
  - Cricket bat edges cracking after extensive use without proper knocking-in
  - Racket string breakage after play (strings are consumable)
  - Grips wearing out (consumable)
  - Punctures in inflatable balls from sharp objects
  - Rust on equipment left exposed to moisture
  - Squeaking or noise from fitness equipment (often resolved with lubrication)
  - Battery drain in fitness trackers (consumable, follow Electronics policy)
  - Discomfort or performance dissatisfaction

1.4 Fitness Equipment (Large Items) Specifics
- Open-Box Delivery is standard for treadmills, exercise bikes, multi-gyms, and weight benches.
- The delivery team will place and assemble (if opted) the equipment. Inspection must be done before signing the POD.
- Manufacturing defects identified during assembly are covered. The assembly team documents the defect.
- Self-assembly damage is NOT covered. Professional assembly is strongly recommended.
- Return of large fitness equipment requires disassembly and original packaging. A specialized logistics team handles pickup.
- Return shipping charges for change-of-mind returns: 15-20% of product value or actual freight cost, whichever is higher. Defect returns have zero charges.

1.5 Wearable Devices (Fitness Trackers, GPS Watches, Heart Rate Monitors)
- Follow the Electronics & Gadgets policy: Replacement Only for genuine defects within 7 days.
- Water damage to devices rated as water-resistant is covered only if usage was within the rated depth and duration, and all port covers were properly sealed.
- Heart rate accuracy complaints are investigated; inherent limitations of optical sensors are not defects.

1.6 Nutrition and Supplements
- Protein powders, pre-workouts, BCAA, creatine, energy bars, and gels: Non-returnable once the safety seal is broken.
- Returnable within 7 days if:
  - Seal is intact and product is unopened
  - Wrong flavor/variant delivered
  - Damaged packaging (torn pouch, dented tub, broken scoop)
  - Expired product at delivery
  - Product with less than 3 months to expiry (unless sold as clearance)
- Authenticity guarantee applies. Counterfeit supplement reports are investigated with manufacturer verification.

1.7 Refund Timelines
- Sports Equipment: 5-7 business days after quality check.
- Fitness Equipment: 7-10 business days after pickup and warehouse inspection.
- Supplements: 3-5 business days after return receipt (unopened) or photo verification (damaged).

SECTION 2: WARRANTY POLICY

2.2 Warranty Claim Process
- Contact HelperTom support with product photos, purchase invoice, and defect description.
- Some brands require direct contact with their service center. Check the warranty card in the product box.

SECTION 5: SPORTS & FITNESS FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: I bought a cricket bat. Can I return it after knocking it in and playing one net session?
A: No. Once a bat has been knocked in or used to hit balls, ball marks and wear are visible, making it non-returnable. Test the bat's pickup and balance at home without hitting any balls. Return it within 7 days only if completely unused.

Q2: My treadmill makes a loud squeaking noise from day one. Is this a defect?
A: Squeaking in new treadmills is often due to belt-deck friction during the break-in period. First, apply the lubricant provided (or silicone treadmill lubricant) as per the manual. If squeaking persists after proper lubrication, report within 10 days with a video. A technician visit will be arranged to inspect and resolve the issue.

Q3: I ordered a pair of 5kg dumbbells, but received 4kg dumbbells. What should I do?
A: Weigh them on a scale and photograph the weight reading against the product. Report within 7 days. If the weight is incorrect (outside ±3% tolerance), it's a wrong product delivery. The correct 5kg dumbbells will be shipped, and the 4kg ones picked up.

Q4: Can I return a badminton racket if the strings break during my first game?
A: If the strings broke during the first game within 7 days of delivery, report it. Our quality team will examine if it was a stringing defect or a mishit (frame contact). Genuine stringing defects are replaced. Mishits causing string breakage are not covered; string breakage during play is generally considered normal wear.

Q5: I bought a yoga mat that has a very strong chemical smell. I've aired it out for 3 days, and it still stinks. Is this returnable?
A: Yes. While new yoga mats have some initial odor, a persistent overpowering chemical smell after airing out indicates a material defect. Report within 7 days with a description. A replacement or refund will be provided. Do not use the mat extensively if you plan to return it.

Q6: What is the return policy for a football that I inflated and kicked around in my backyard?
A: Once a ball has been inflated and used (scuff marks, dirt, grass stains), it is non-returnable. You can inspect the ball's appearance, feel the material, and check the bladder by partially inflating it, but outdoor use makes it ineligible.

Q7: My protein powder tub arrived with a broken safety seal. Can I return it even if I opened the outer box?
A: Yes. If the inner safety seal under the lid is broken or missing when you first open the tub, photograph it immediately and report within 48 hours. Do not consume the product. A full replacement or refund will be issued. Never consume supplements with a broken seal.

Q8: Can I return an exercise bike if I find it uncomfortable and the seat hurts?
A: Exercise bike seats often feel firm to new users. This is not a defect. Comfort is subjective and improves with time and padded shorts. You cannot return an assembled exercise bike for comfort reasons. Consider purchasing a gel seat cover separately.

Q9: I received a cricket helmet with a cracked visor. What should I do?
A: Report within 48 hours with clear photos of the crack. A damaged helmet is a safety hazard. We will replace the entire helmet. Do not use it even once.

Q10: My GPS running watch shows a heart rate that's clearly wrong compared to my chest strap. Is it defective?
A: Optical wrist-based heart rate sensors have inherent accuracy limitations compared to chest straps, especially during interval training or activities with wrist flexion. This is a technology limitation, not a product defect. However, if the watch shows wildly inaccurate readings (e.g., 180 bpm at rest), report within 7 days for evaluation.

Q11: I assembled my weight bench myself, and now it wobbles. The bolts are tight. What do I do?
A: Check if the floor is level. If the bench wobbles on a flat surface despite tight bolts, it's a manufacturing defect. Report within 10 days with a video showing the wobble. If self-assembly error is suspected, a technician can be sent (charges apply if it's assembly error).

Q12: Can I return a skipping rope if the handles are uncomfortable in my hands?
A: Comfort preference is not a return reason once packaging is opened. However, if the handles are cracked, the cable is frayed, or the bearing doesn't rotate smoothly out of the box, it's a defect and returnable within 7 days.

Q13: I bought a pair of running shoes. After running 5km, I have blisters. Can I return them?
A: No. Once shoes are worn outdoors for running, they are non-returnable (follows Footwear policy). Blisters can result from fit, sock choice, or running form, which are not product defects. Always break in new running shoes gradually.

Q14: My multi-gym cable snapped during the first week of use. Is this covered?
A: Yes. A cable snapping within the return window under normal use is a serious defect and potential safety hazard. Stop using the equipment immediately. Report within 10 days with photos. A replacement cable or full unit replacement will be provided.

Q15: The table tennis bat I received has a peeled rubber edge. Is it defective?
A: A slightly lifted rubber edge is a defect. Report within 7 days with photos. A replacement bat will be shipped. This can happen during manufacturing or transit.

Q16: Can I return an unopened box of energy bars if I decided to switch to a different brand?
A: Yes, if the box is completely sealed, within 7 days, and the bars are not near expiry. Opened boxes are non-returnable.

Q17: I purchased a basketball hoop that arrived with a bent rim. What is the process?
A: Report within 48 hours with photos of the bent rim during or after delivery. Do not attempt to bend it back. A replacement rim or full unit replacement will be shipped.

Q18: My foam roller has dents and doesn't roll smoothly right out of the box. Is this normal?
A: Foam rollers should be smooth and cylindrical when new. Dents and an uneven surface indicate a defect. Report within 7 days for a replacement. Some EVA foam rollers have a textured surface by design — this is different from dents.

Q19: Can I return a fitness tracker if the battery only lasts 2 days instead of the advertised 7 days?
A: Battery life varies based on usage (GPS, always-on display, notifications). Test with typical settings. If the battery drains abnormally fast (e.g., 50% in an hour with GPS off), report within 7 days. This qualifies as a defect under the Electronics policy.

Q20: I bought a cricket kit bag, and the zipper broke on the second day. Can I return just the bag?
A: Yes, a broken zipper on a new bag is a defect. If the bag was part of a kit, we may replace just the bag or the entire kit depending on how it was sold. Report within 7 days with a photo of the broken zipper.

Q21: My resistance band snapped on the very first stretch. I wasn't even stretching it to the max. Is this covered?
A: Yes, a band snapping during initial, proper use indicates a material defect. Report within 7 days with a photo of the snapped band. Do not tie it and continue using it. A replacement or refund will be provided.

Q22: Can I return an abdominal wheel if I find the exercise too difficult to perform?
A: No. Difficulty of exercise is not a product defect. The product is functional; the challenge is inherent to the exercise. We recommend starting with easier core exercises.

Q23: I received a punching bag without the hanging chain and brackets. What do I do?
A: Photograph all received items and compare with the "In the Box" list on the product page. Report the missing parts within 7 days. We will ship the missing hardware kit. Do not attempt to hang the bag with improvised hardware.

Q24: What is the return policy for swim fins and goggles if they don't fit?
A: Swim goggles and fins are non-returnable once the hygiene seal is broken and they've been tried on (contact with skin/water). Size guides are provided; please measure carefully before ordering.

Q25: My elliptical machine's display console is blank and won't turn on. Is it DOA?
A: First, check the power connections, the adapter, and try a different wall outlet. If it still doesn't power on, it is Dead on Arrival. Report within 10 days. A technician will be sent to diagnose. If confirmed DOA, the console or the entire unit will be replaced.

Q26: I bought sports gloves (batting gloves, gym gloves) and they're too tight. Can I exchange for a larger size?
A: Yes, if the gloves are unworn, with tags intact, and no signs of stretching or sweat. An exchange for a different size is allowed within 7 days. Worn gloves, even briefly, are non-returnable for hygiene reasons.

Q27: Can I return a set of golf clubs if I went to the driving range once and didn't like them?
A: No. Golf clubs used at a range will show ball marks and turf scratches on the clubface and sole. These are non-returnable once used. We recommend testing clubs at a pro shop before purchasing.

Q28: The water bottle I ordered for my gym use arrived with a crack. Can I get a refund?
A: Yes. Photograph the crack and report within 48 hours. A leaking/cracked water bottle is a defect. A replacement or refund will be provided.

Q29: I bought a weighted vest, and the stitching is coming undone after one workout. Is this returnable?
A: Yes, stitching coming undone after minimal use is a manufacturing defect. Report within 7 days with photos. A replacement will be shipped. Do not continue using a vest with compromised stitching as weights could fall out.

Q30: My new treadmill's belt keeps slipping when I run. I've adjusted the tension as per the manual. What now?
A: If the belt slips despite proper tensioning per the manual, there may be a motor-pulley issue or a defective belt. Report within 10 days with a video. A technician visit will be scheduled. Do not continue using a slipping treadmill as it's a safety risk.
