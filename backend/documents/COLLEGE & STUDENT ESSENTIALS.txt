SECTION 1: RETURNS & REFUND POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Laptops, Tablets, and Computing Devices: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects; follows Electronics & Gadgets policy).
- Stationery and Study Supplies: 7 days from delivery.
- Textbooks, Reference Books, and Study Guides: 7 days (Replacement Only for defective/wrong items; follows Books & Stationery policy).
- Backpacks, Laptop Bags, and Messenger Bags: 7 days.
- Dorm Room Essentials (Bedding, Organizers, Lamps, Small Furniture): 7 days.
- Calculators (Scientific, Financial, Graphing): 7 days (Replacement Only for defects).
- Lab Coats, Aprons, and Safety Gear: 7 days (unused, with tags).
- Student ID Holders, Lanyards, and Accessories: 7 days.
- Educational Kits, Project Supplies, and DIY Electronics: 7 days.
- Planners, Journals, and Academic Diaries: 7 days (unused — no writing).
- Art and Design Supplies: 7 days.
- Hostel Kitchen Appliances (Electric Kettles, Induction Cooktops, Small Fridges): 7 days (follows Kitchen Appliances policy; Replacement Only for defects on electronics).
- Online Course Access Codes and Digital Learning Materials: Non-returnable once accessed or revealed.
- Customized/Personalized Student Items: Non-returnable.

1.2 Condition for Returns
- Stationery: Unused. Notebooks and planners with any writing, highlighting, or erased marks are non-returnable.
- Backpacks and Bags: Must be unused, with tags. Bags that have been loaded with books or carried show creasing, stretching, or marks and will be rejected.
- Dorm Bedding: Must be unopened in original packaging. Opened bedding (sheets, pillows, comforters) that has been slept on or contacted skin is non-returnable for hygiene reasons.
- Lamps and Small Furniture: Must be unused, unassembled (or in original assembled condition if delivered assembled). Follows Furniture & Home Decor policy.
- Calculators: Replacement Only. Must have all protective films. Used or programmed calculators are non-returnable.
- Lab Coats and Safety Gear: Unworn, with tags. Once worn in a lab/workshop environment, non-returnable for safety/hygiene.
- Educational Kits: Unopened or with all components intact. Kits with opened component bags, assembled parts, or used materials are non-returnable.
- Art Supplies: Unopened packaging. Opened paints, canvases, brushes, or used sketchbooks are non-returnable.

1.3 Student Budget and Discount Considerations
- HelperTom offers a Student Discount Program. Verified students receive exclusive discounts on select categories.
- Student discount purchases are subject to the same return policies as regular purchases.
- Bulk orders for student groups, hostels, or college events: Contact our Institutional Sales team for custom quotes and return policies.

1.4 Defective Product Policy
- Defects covered:
  - Laptops/tablets: DOA, hardware failure, screen defects (follows Electronics policy)
  - Calculators: Malfunctioning display, unresponsive keys, calculation errors
  - Backpacks: Torn seams, broken zippers, defective buckles
  - Dorm essentials: Broken desk lamps, malfunctioning alarm clocks, defective power strips
  - Educational kits: Missing components, defective electronic parts
  - Lab coats: Torn seams, broken buttons, incorrect size delivered
  - Stationery: Misprinted planners (wrong dates), defective binding on notebooks
  - Art supplies: Dried-out markers, dried-out paint in sealed tubes, defective brushes

- Defects NOT covered:
  - "I dropped my new laptop" — physical damage (buy a protective case and consider accidental damage protection)
  - Spilled coffee on a textbook or notebook — liquid damage is user-caused
  - Lost or stolen items — keep track of your belongings
  - "The calculator doesn't have the functions I need" — check specifications before buying
  - "My roommate borrowed and damaged it" — user responsibility
  - Normal backpack wear from heavy textbooks — choose a bag rated for the weight you carry

1.5 Refund Timelines
- Standard returns: 5-7 business days after quality check.
- Electronic items (defect replacement): 5-10 business days for replacement; refund if replacement unavailable.
- Student discount orders: Refund back to original payment method including the discounted amount.

SECTION 5: COLLEGE & STUDENT ESSENTIALS FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: I bought a scientific calculator for my engineering entrance exam. It arrived with a non-functional display — half the screen is blank. What should I do?
A: Photograph the defective display and report within 7 days. A replacement calculator will be shipped immediately. For exam-critical purchases, we recommend ordering at least 2 weeks in advance to allow time for replacement if needed. Non-functional display is a clear DOA defect.

Q2: Can I return a backpack if I put my books in it once and the shoulder strap padding feels too thin?
A: Once loaded and worn, a backpack shows signs of use (strap creasing, slight fabric stretching) and may be rejected during quality check. Test the backpack empty first. Check the padding specifications in the product description. If you haven't removed tags or used the backpack, you can return it within 7 days. Comfort is subjective and best assessed before use.

Q3: I ordered the wrong edition of a textbook for my course. The professor specified the 10th edition, and I bought the 9th. Can I return it?
A: If the book is unopened (still in shrink-wrap) or in pristine, unmarked condition, you can return it within 7 days. If you've opened it, written in it, or the access code is revealed, it is non-returnable per the Books policy. Always verify the exact ISBN with your course syllabus before ordering.

Q4: My new laptop for college has a keyboard issue — the 'E' key requires extra force to register. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. A keyboard with inconsistent key actuation is a manufacturing defect. Test all keys systematically. Report within 7 days with a video showing the problematic key. A replacement laptop will be provided. This is covered under the Electronics replacement policy.

Q5: Can I return a set of highlighters if one color is dried out?
A: Yes. A dried-out highlighter in a new set is a defect. Photograph the dried-out highlighter tip and a scribble test. Report within 7 days. A replacement set will be shipped. For multi-color sets, we typically replace the entire set to ensure you have all colors.

Q6: I bought a dorm room desk lamp. It flickers constantly and gives me a headache when studying. Is this returnable?
A: Constant flickering (beyond the normal warm-up of fluorescent lamps) is a defect — likely a faulty LED driver or loose connection. Report within 7 days with a video showing the flicker. A replacement lamp will be shipped. Eye strain from flickering lights is a real study impediment, and we take it seriously.

Q7: My academic planner has the wrong months (starts from July instead of January as I ordered). Can I exchange it?
A: Yes. If you ordered an academic-year planner (specific start month) and received a different one, it's a wrong product. Photograph the planner showing the incorrect months. Report within 7 days. The correct planner will be shipped. Do not write in the incorrect planner.

Q8: Can I return an online course access code if the course content is not what I expected?
A: No. Digital access codes are non-returnable and non-refundable once the code is revealed or the course is accessed. Course descriptions and syllabi are available before purchase. This is an industry-standard policy to prevent piracy and "consume and refund" scenarios.

Q9: I bought a lab coat in size Medium based on the size chart, but it's too small. Can I exchange for a Large?
A: Yes, if the lab coat is unworn, with all tags, and within 7 days. Try it on over your regular clothes (not in a lab setting) to check fit. Once worn in a lab, it's non-returnable for safety reasons. Size exchanges are allowed for unworn lab coats.

Q10: My portable hard drive for backing up college work is not recognized by my laptop. I've tried multiple ports and another computer. Is it DOA?
A: Ensure the drive is getting power (some require a powered USB port or Y-cable). Try Disk Management (Windows) or Disk Utility (Mac) to see if it's detected but not initialized. If completely unrecognized across multiple devices, it's DOA. Report within 7 days. A replacement will be shipped. Follow the Electronics policy.

Q11: I bought a set of geometry tools (compass, protractor, set squares). The compass joint is loose and won't hold a radius. Is this defective?
A: Yes. A compass that can't hold its setting is functionally defective — it cannot draw accurate circles. Report within 7 days with a video showing the loose joint. A replacement geometry set will be shipped.

Q12: Can I return a whiteboard that arrived with a dent and a scratch on the surface?
A: Yes. A dented or scratched whiteboard surface affects writing and erasing. Photograph the damage and report within 48 hours. A replacement whiteboard will be shipped. This is transit/manufacturing damage and is fully covered.

Q13: My noise-cancelling headphones for library study have a constant static hiss when ANC is on. Is this normal?
A: A very faint hiss can be the ANC circuit's noise floor. However, if the static is loud, distracting, or present even during audio playback, it's a defect. Report within 7 days with a recording. A replacement headphone set will be provided.

Q14: I ordered a hostel mattress topper. It arrived with a strong chemical/foam smell. I need to sleep on it tonight. Is it safe?
A: New foam products (mattress toppers, mattresses) often have a "new foam" odor from manufacturing. This is off-gassing and typically dissipates within 24-72 hours when aired out. Unroll the topper and air it in a well-ventilated room. If the odor is unbearable or persists beyond 3-4 days of airing, report within 7 days. Safety is paramount — if you feel unwell, do not sleep on it and contact us immediately.

Q15: Can I return a set of colored pencils if the leads keep breaking when I sharpen them?
A: Leads repeatedly breaking during sharpening often indicate the pencils were dropped during transit, causing internal lead fractures. Try a different sharpener first. If multiple pencils from the set break consistently, report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement set will be provided.

Q16: I bought a desk organizer. One of the compartments is cracked. The box was undamaged. Can I get a replacement?
A: Yes. A crack in a new product with undamaged packaging is a manufacturing defect. Photograph the crack and report within 7 days. A replacement organizer will be shipped.

Q17: My power strip for my hostel room has a loose socket — plugs keep falling out. Is this a defect or a safety hazard?
A: A loose socket is both a defect and a potential safety hazard (arcing, overheating). Stop using the power strip immediately. Report within 7 days with a video showing the loose connection. A replacement will be shipped with priority. Electrical safety is critical.

Q18: Can I return an opened pack of loose-leaf paper if I used 5 sheets and decided I don't like the paper quality?
A: No. Opened and partially used stationery is non-returnable. Paper quality (GSM, ruled/unruled, margin style) is listed on the product page. Buy a smaller pack first if you're trying a new brand.

Q19: I ordered a graphing calculator for my calculus class. It powers on but gets stuck on the boot screen. Is it defective?
A: Yes. A calculator that won't boot past the startup screen has a firmware or hardware failure. Try a hard reset (check manual for the key combination). If it still doesn't boot, report within 7 days. A replacement will be shipped.

Q20: My messenger bag's zipper broke on the third day of use. I didn't overstuff it. Can I get a replacement?
A: Yes. A zipper breaking under normal use within days is a clear defect. Photograph the broken zipper. Report within 7 days. A replacement bag will be shipped. Zippers on quality bags should last for years, not days.

Q21: I bought a poster for my dorm room wall. It arrived with a crease down the middle from being folded. Can I return it?
A: Posters should arrive rolled in a tube, not folded. A crease from folding is packaging damage. Report within 48 hours with a photo. A replacement poster (shipped in a tube) will be sent. Ensure you select the "tube shipping" option if available for posters.

Q22: Can I return an unopened box of engineering drawing sheets if I switched to a digital drawing tablet for my coursework?
A: Yes. Unopened stationery can be returned within 7 days for change of mind. Once the packaging is opened, it's non-returnable.

Q23: My study table wobbles even after I tightened all the screws. The floor is level. Is it defective?
A: Yes. A table that wobbles on a level floor despite proper assembly indicates uneven legs or a twisted frame — a manufacturing defect. Report within 7 days (10 days for furniture) with a video showing the wobble. A replacement table or leg set will be provided.

Q24: I bought a digital voice recorder for lectures. The recorded audio has a lot of static and the professor's voice is barely audible. Is it defective?
A: Check the recording settings (sensitivity, mic direction). Ensure the mic isn't covered and you're within the reasonable range (typically 10-15 feet for built-in mics). If audio quality is extremely poor even in ideal conditions (quiet room, close range), report within 7 days with a sample recording. The microphone may be defective.

Q25: Can I return a college ID card holder if the lanyard clip broke the first time I put it on?
A: Yes. A broken clip on first use is a defect. Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement will be shipped. It's a small item, but you need it to work.

Q26: My laptop cooling pad's fan makes a rattling noise and doesn't cool my laptop. Is this a defect?
A: A rattling noise indicates a fan bearing defect or a foreign object. It's defective. Report within 7 days with a video capturing the noise. A replacement cooling pad will be shipped. An ineffective cooling pad with a noisy fan is a double defect.

Q27: I ordered a chemistry lab coat, but I received a biology lab coat. They look different. Is this a wrong product?
A: If the listing and your order specify a particular type of lab coat and you received a different type, it's a wrong product. Report within 7 days with a photo. The correct lab coat will be shipped. If both are "white lab coats" and the type wasn't specified, variations may exist.

Q28: Can I return a set of brush pens if the colors on the caps don't match the actual ink color?
A: Significant cap-to-ink color mismatch across multiple pens in a set is a manufacturing/quality control defect. Report within 7 days with a photo showing the mismatch (e.g., a green-capped pen that writes brown). A replacement set will be provided. Slight variations are expected.

Q29: My hostel room's mini-fridge is making a loud clunking sound and isn't cooling. The delivery was 5 days ago. What should I do?
A: A loud clunking sound from a refrigerator compressor, combined with no cooling, indicates a compressor or relay defect. Report within 10 days. A technician will inspect, and the unit will be replaced. Do not attempt to tip or shake the fridge. This follows the Kitchen Appliances policy.

Q30: I bought a 5-subject notebook. When I opened it, one entire subject section has blank pages (no ruling). The rest are ruled. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. A mixed-ruling notebook that's supposed to be uniformly ruled (or with consistent section formatting) has a manufacturing binding error. Report within 7 days with a photo showing the blank section. A replacement notebook will be provided. Don't write in it if you plan to return it.
