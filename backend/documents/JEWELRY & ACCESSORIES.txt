SECTION 1: RETURNS & REFUND POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Jewelry & Accessories are eligible for return within 7 days from the date of delivery.
- Fine Jewelry (Gold, Diamond, Platinum, Silver): 7-day return window with mandatory quality verification.
- Fashion/Costume Jewelry: 7-day return window, subject to condition and hygiene checks.
- Precious metal coins and bars: Non-returnable once delivered.

1.2 Condition for Returns
- Jewelry must be returned in its original condition with the original branded box, certificate of authenticity, warranty card, and all accompanying documentation.
- All security tags, barcode stickers, and tamper-evident seals must be intact.
- Earrings, nose pins, and all piercings are non-returnable for hygiene reasons unless defective or wrong item delivered.
- Watches must have the protective films, crown stopper, and tags intact. Watch bracelets must not be resized, and any removed links must be included.
- Sunglasses and eyewear must be returned with the original case, cleaning cloth, authenticity card, and all accessories. The lenses must be unscratched and frames unbent.
- Belts must be unbuckled from the original position; any new holes punched make the item non-returnable.
- Bags and wallets must have all protective covers, stuffing, and tags intact with no signs of use.
- Scarves, stoles, caps, and hats must be unworn with tags attached.

1.3 Non-Returnable Jewelry & Accessories
The following items are strictly non-returnable for hygiene, safety, and security reasons:
- Earrings, ear cuffs, nose pins, nose rings, and all piercing jewelry
- Body jewelry of any kind
- Precious metal coins and bars (Gold, Silver, Platinum)
- Customized, engraved, or personalized jewelry
- Items with broken security seals or tags
- Loose gemstones (unless damaged or wrong stone delivered)
- Gift cards and e-vouchers

1.4 Fine Jewelry (Gold, Diamond, Platinum, Silver) Specifics
- All Fine Jewelry returns undergo a mandatory quality verification at a certified jewelry appraisal center.
- Verification includes:
  - Purity testing (karat meter for gold, XRF analysis)
  - Weight verification against original dispatch record
  - Diamond/gemstone verification (carat, clarity, color, cut match)
  - Hallmark and certification verification
  - Tamper/alteration check
- The verification process takes 5-7 business days post pickup.
- If the jewelry passes verification (unaltered, matching dispatch records), the refund is processed within 2-3 business days.
- If discrepancies are found (weight change, stone replacement, purity mismatch), the return is rejected, and legal action may be pursued.
- Customers must retain the original purchase invoice and certificate until the refund is completed.

1.5 Hallmark and Certification
- All gold jewelry sold on HelperTom is BIS hallmarked as per Indian regulations.
- Diamond jewelry (0.30 carat and above) comes with a grading certificate from a recognized gemological laboratory (IGI, GIA, GSI, etc.).
- Solitaire diamonds come with individual certificates. Loose solitaire diamonds are non-returnable unless the certificate details do not match the stone.
- Customers who suspect purity or certification fraud can request an independent assay. If the independent assay confirms a discrepancy, HelperTom provides a 2X refund of the product value.

1.6 Watch Policy
- Watches operate on a 7-day replacement guarantee, similar to Electronics.
- Dead on Arrival, manufacturing defects, and wrong variants are covered.
- Returns for "change of mind" are not accepted if the protective films or tags are removed.
- Leather watch straps that have been worn or show sweat marks are non-returnable.
- Smartwatches and hybrid watches follow the Electronics & Gadgets policy for functionality issues and the Jewelry policy for cosmetic concerns.
- Automatic and mechanical watches have an acceptable time deviation of -20 to +40 seconds per day as per industry standards. Deviation within this range is not a defect.
- Chronograph functions must be tested and reported for defects within 7 days.

1.7 Sunglasses and Eyewear
- Sunglasses with prescription lenses (custom made) are non-returnable.
- Non-prescription sunglasses can be returned within 7 days if unworn, with original case, cloth, and tags.
- Scratched lenses, bent frames, or missing nose pads lead to rejection.

1.8 Handbags, Wallets, and Leather Goods
- Returnable within 7 days if unused with all protective covers and stuffing intact.
- Signs of use include: scratches on hardware, marks on leather, filled card slots, perfume odors, and stretched pockets.
- Exotic leather bags (python, crocodile, ostrich) require CITES documentation. These are non-returnable once the CITES tag is removed.

1.9 Refund Timelines
- Fashion Jewelry: 5-7 business days after quality check.
- Fine Jewelry: 2-3 business days after jewelry verification (total 7-10 business days from pickup).
- Prepaid orders: Refund to source. COD orders: Bank transfer.

SECTION 2: WARRANTY AND LIFETIME SERVICES
----------------------------------------------

2.1 Jewelry Warranty
- Fashion jewelry does not carry a warranty beyond the 7-day return window.
- Fine Jewelry from select brands includes lifetime maintenance services (cleaning, polishing, rhodium plating) at authorized stores.
- Diamond jewelry often includes a buyback or exchange policy from the brand. Check the product page and certificate for details.
- Gold jewelry may be eligible for exchange at prevailing market rates per the brand's policy.

2.2 Watch Warranty
- Watches carry the manufacturer's international or India-specific warranty (typically 1-2 years).
- Warranty covers movement defects, not external damage, wear and tear, or battery replacement (for quartz watches).
- Water damage to water-resistant watches is covered only if the crown was properly screwed down and usage was within the depth rating. Evidence of crown misuse voids the warranty.

2.3 Lifetime Service
- Some premium accessory brands offer lifetime cleaning, polishing, or maintenance. Details are in the warranty booklet. HelperTom does not manage this service directly.

- Made-to-order or customized pieces: Cancellation only within 2 hours of order placement and only if production has not started.

SECTION 5: JEWELRY & ACCESSORIES FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: Can I return gold earrings if they don't suit my face?
A: No. Earrings, nose pins, and all piercing jewelry are non-returnable for hygiene reasons, regardless of whether they are fashion or fine jewelry. We recommend trying similar styles at a physical store or using virtual try-on tools before purchasing.

Q2: I bought a diamond ring online. The diamond looks smaller than in the photos. Can I return it?
A: Diamond size is measured by carat weight, not visual appearance. The carat weight is certified and verifiable. If the carat weight on the certificate matches what you ordered, size perception is not a valid return reason. If you believe the carat weight is misrepresented, you can request an independent appraisal. If confirmed, you receive a 2X refund.

Q3: The gold chain I received has a different weight than the product page mentioned. What should I do?
A: Gold jewelry weight can vary slightly (±2-3%) due to manufacturing tolerances and finishing. The invoiced weight is the actual weight. If the discrepancy is more than 3% from the listed weight, report within 48 hours with a weighing photo. We will investigate and adjust the invoice or replace the item.

Q4: Can I exchange a silver bracelet for a gold one and pay the difference?
A: No. Exchanges across different metals, designs, or price points are not supported. You can return the silver bracelet (if eligible) for a refund and place a new order for the gold bracelet.

Q5: I received a branded watch, but the box looks different from the product images. Is this a problem?
A: Watch brands sometimes update packaging without notice. If the watch is genuine, has the correct model number, and comes with a valid warranty card, packaging changes are not considered defects. If you suspect counterfeit, contact our Authenticity Team.

Q6: My automatic watch stops working overnight even after wearing it all day. Is this a defect?
A: Automatic watches have a power reserve (typically 38-48 hours). If it stops overnight despite being worn for 8+ hours, the movement may have an issue. Report within 7 days. Our service center will test the power reserve; if below the manufacturer's specification, it will be replaced.

Q7: Can I return a bracelet with a broken clasp that arrived damaged?
A: Yes. A broken clasp is a manufacturing defect. Photograph the damage clearly and report within 48 hours of delivery. A replacement or full refund will be provided.

Q8: What happens if my returned fine jewelry gets lost during the return transit?
A: All fine jewelry returns are fully insured. If the package is lost in transit, HelperTom bears the liability, and you will receive a full refund. We track all insured shipments in real-time.

Q9: I ordered a pair of sunglasses, and they don't fit my face well. Can I return them?
A: Yes, if the sunglasses are unworn, with the case, cloth, and tags intact, and within the 7-day window. Fit issues are accepted for sunglasses, unlike prescription eyewear.

Q10: Can I return a leather handbag if the leather has a slight scratch right out of the box?
A: A visible scratch on a new bag is a defect. Photograph and report within 48 hours. Minor natural leather variations (veins, grain patterns) are not defects and are characteristic of genuine leather.

Q11: I bought a pearl necklace, and the pearls have slight irregularities. Is this normal?
A: Yes. Real pearls — especially freshwater and cultured pearls — are organic gems with natural surface variations, slight shape irregularities, and luster differences. This is not a defect but a sign of authenticity. If the pearls were described as perfectly round and flawless and they are clearly not, that is a misrepresentation and eligible for return.

Q12: Can I return a personalized name necklace?
A: No. All customized, personalized, engraved, or monogrammed jewelry is made to order and is non-returnable and non-exchangeable unless there is a manufacturing defect or the personalization is incorrect (wrong name/spelling).

Q13: My silver jewelry has started tarnishing after a week. Is it defective?
A: Silver tarnishing is a natural chemical reaction to air, moisture, and body chemistry. It is not a defect. Silver requires regular cleaning and proper storage. Tarnish can be removed with a silver polishing cloth. This is not covered under the return policy.

Q14: I bought a necklace set during a Diwali sale. Can I return it?
A: Sale items follow the same return policy unless specifically marked "Final Sale" or "Non-Returnable." Check the product page. Fine jewelry purchased during sales retains its return eligibility and certification requirements.

Q15: What is the return policy for men's accessories like cufflinks and tie pins?
A: Men's accessories follow the standard 7-day return policy. They must be unused, with original packaging. Cufflinks and tie pins are not subject to the piercing jewelry restrictions and can be returned for change of mind.

Q16: My watch's chronograph second hand doesn't reset to zero. Is this covered?
A: Yes. A chronograph that doesn't reset properly is a movement defect. Report within 7 days with a video. This is covered under the replacement guarantee.

Q17: Can I return a fashion necklace if the plating has worn off after two wears?
A: Plating wear after minimal use may indicate a quality issue. Report within 7 days with photos. Fashion jewelry plating is expected to last more than two wears. If the quality team confirms premature wear, a refund or replacement will be issued.

Q18: I received diamond jewelry, but the certificate is missing. What should I do?
A: Diamonds above 0.30 carat must be accompanied by a grading certificate. If missing, report within 48 hours. We will arrange for the certificate to be sent or, if unavailable, accept a return and full refund.

Q19: Can I get my gold jewelry tested at an independent lab?
A: Yes. You are free to get an independent assay. If the assay confirms purity lower than the hallmark, HelperTom will provide a 2X refund and cover the assay cost. The independent lab must be NABL accredited.

Q20: I bought a watch online and found it cheaper elsewhere the next day. Can I get a price match refund?
A: HelperTom does not offer a price match guarantee. If you find it cheaper elsewhere, you can return the watch (if eligible and unopened/unworn) and purchase from the other retailer. Price drops on our platform after your purchase are not compensated.

Q21: The belt I ordered is too long. Can I return it after punching extra holes?
A: No. Altering a belt by punching holes makes it non-returnable. You can return an unaltered belt for a size exchange or refund. We recommend confirming belt size (waist measurement in inches) before ordering.

Q22: What is the return policy for gold coins?
A: Gold coins, silver coins, and precious metal bars are non-returnable once delivered. This is an industry-wide standard due to price volatility and purity verification challenges. Check the product and seal integrity at delivery.

Q23: I received a designer bag, but the dust bag inside is of a different brand. Is that suspicious?
A: Yes, this is a red flag. Designer bags should come with the brand's own dust bag, authenticity card, and care booklet. Report immediately. This will be investigated as a potential counterfeit or packaging error.

Q24: My tennis bracelet's clasp keeps opening on its own. Is it defective?
A: Yes. A faulty clasp that doesn't secure properly is a defect and a safety risk (you could lose the bracelet). Report within 7 days with a video showing the issue. A replacement or repair will be offered.

Q25: Can I return earrings if they came with a broken back/finding?
A: Yes. Broken findings (butterfly backs, push backs, screw backs) on an otherwise non-returnable item due to hygiene are still eligible for replacement or refund as it is a clear defect. Report within 48 hours with photos.

Q26: I bought a gemstone ring, and the stone looks cloudy, not like the photo. Can I return it?
A: Gemstone color and clarity vary between individual stones. The photo is representative. If the stone delivered is significantly cloudier or lower quality than the advertised grade (e.g., advertised as "eye-clean" but has visible inclusions), you can raise a dispute. Our gemologist will review.

Q27: What happens if a watch I ordered has a dead battery out of the box?
A: A quartz watch that is dead on arrival is covered. Report within 7 days. Do not open the case back or attempt to change the battery yourself, as this voids the warranty. We will replace the watch.

Q28: Can I return a rosary or religious pendant if I changed my mind?
A: Rosaries, religious pendants, and sacred items are treated as standard jewelry for return purposes unless explicitly marked non-returnable. However, out of respect for customer sentiments, they must be unused with tags and packaging intact within the 7-day window.

Q29: I ordered a 22K gold bangle, but the hallmark shows 18K. What should I do?
A: This is a serious discrepancy. Photograph the hallmark clearly and report within 48 hours. This qualifies as a "Wrong Product / Misrepresentation." A full refund or replacement with the correct purity will be provided immediately upon verification.

Q30: My gold jewelry order arrived with the security seal broken. Should I accept it?
A: If the tamper-evident security seal is broken or missing at delivery, refuse acceptance and immediately report to HelperTom. Do not accept a high-value package with a broken seal. If you accepted it and opened it later to find an issue, your claim becomes much harder to verify. Unboxing videos are critical here.
