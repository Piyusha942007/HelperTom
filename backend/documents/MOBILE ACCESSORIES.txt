SECTION 1: RETURNS & REPLACEMENT POLICY
----------------------------------------------

1.1 Return Window
- Mobile Cases and Covers: 7 days from delivery.
- Screen Protectors and Tempered Glass: 7 days (unused, unapplied only).
- Chargers, Cables, and Adapters: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects).
- Power Banks: 7 days (Replacement Only for defects; follows Electronics policy).
- Earphones, Headphones, and Earbuds (Wired and Wireless): 7 days (Replacement Only for defects; hygiene restrictions apply).
- Mobile Holders, Stands, and Mounts: 7 days.
- Pop Sockets, Grips, and Rings: 7 days.
- Memory Cards and OTG Drives: 7 days (Replacement Only; follow Electronics policy).
- Selfie Sticks and Tripods: 7 days.
- Smartwatch Straps and Bands: 7 days (unused, hygiene restrictions).
- Stylus Pens: 7 days.

1.2 Condition for Returns
- Mobile Cases and Covers: Must be unused. Any sign of installation (stretching, scratches on inner felt) leads to rejection. The original packaging must be intact.
- Screen Protectors: Must be in sealed original packaging. Opened or applied screen protectors are non-returnable (even if applied poorly or with bubbles).
- Tempered Glass: Same as screen protectors — must be sealed. Applied tempered glass with bubbles, misalignment, or cracks from application is non-returnable.
- Chargers and Cables: Must be in original packaging with all accessories. Cut or stripped cables are non-returnable.
- Earphones and Headphones: Hygiene restrictions apply. In-ear monitors, earbuds, and ear-tip products are non-returnable once the seal is broken unless defective. Over-ear headphones can be returned if unused with all protective films intact.
- Power Banks: Must be in original packaging. Used power banks are non-returnable for change of mind.
- Memory Cards: Must be unformatted and without stored data. We can verify write cycles.
- Smartwatch Straps: Unworn with tags. Stretching, sweat marks, or wear leads to rejection.

1.3 Defective Product Policy
- Defects covered:
  - Charger not working (no output) with multiple devices and cables
  - Cable not charging or transferring data
  - Power bank not charging devices or not holding charge
  - Earphones/headphones with no sound, distorted audio, or one side not working
  - Bluetooth accessories that don't pair or have intermittent disconnection within range
  - Memory card not recognized by multiple devices, data corruption, or incorrect capacity
  - Case with manufacturing defects: misaligned cutouts, sharp edges, peeling finish
  - Tempered glass with adhesive failure (won't stick) despite proper cleaning
  - Phone mount/stand with broken joints or non-functional suction
  - Stylus not working with compatible devices

- Defects NOT covered:
  - Charger/cable damage from bending, pulling, or liquid exposure
  - Charging speed below expectation (unless the charger is rated for a specific protocol and fails to deliver it)
  - Earphone sound quality not meeting personal preference
  - Bluetooth range issues beyond the advertised distance or through multiple walls
  - Memory card speed class not meeting the needs of a specific device (check device compatibility)
  - Case discoloration from extended use (normal wear)
  - Screen protector bubbles or misalignment from self-application
  - Broken tempered glass after a phone drop (screen protector did its job)
  - Accessory incompatibility with a non-standard or modified device

1.4 Hygiene Restrictions (Critical for Mobile Accessories)
The following items are strictly non-returnable once the packaging seal is broken, except for confirmed defects:
- All in-ear earphones, earbuds, and IEMs (In-Ear Monitors)
- Ear tips, ear cushions, and ear hooks
- Smartwatch straps and bands
- Finger sleeves for mobile gaming
- Phone cases that contact skin directly (if packaging seal broken)

1.5 Replacement Process for Defective Accessories
- Step 1: Customer reports the defect within 7 days with a detailed description and, for functional issues, a video.
- Step 2: For select items, a technical troubleshooting call or video call may be conducted to confirm the defect.
- Step 3: Once the defect is confirmed, a return pickup is scheduled.
- Step 4: The product undergoes inspection (2-3 business days).
- Step 5: A new replacement is shipped. If out of stock, a refund is processed.

1.6 Refund Timelines
- Accessories returned for change of mind (where allowed): 5-7 business days after quality check.
- Defective accessories (confirmed): Replacement shipped in 3-5 business days, or refund in 5-7 business days if replacement is unavailable.

SECTION 5: MOBILE ACCESSORIES FAQ (30 QUESTIONS)
----------------------------------------------

Q1: I bought a phone case, but it's too tight and I'm afraid I'll damage my phone trying to remove it. Can I return it?
A: If the case has been installed on the phone, it's considered used and is non-returnable. If you realized the fit was wrong before installation (visually compared), you can return it within 7 days if the packaging is intact. Always test-fit a case gently without fully snapping it on if you're unsure.

Q2: I applied a tempered glass screen protector, and there are bubbles everywhere. I followed the instructions. Can I get a replacement?
A: No. Once applied, screen protectors are non-returnable, regardless of the application result. Bubbles typically result from dust particles on the screen during application or uneven pressure. We recommend having screen protectors professionally applied at a mobile accessory store if you're not confident in self-application. Some brands offer "bubble-free" guarantees directly.

Q3: My new charging cable works intermittently — I have to hold it at a certain angle for my phone to charge. Is it defective?
A: Yes. Intermittent charging requiring you to hold the cable at a specific angle indicates a broken wire or loose connector — a clear defect. Report within 7 days with a video showing the issue. A replacement cable will be shipped. Do not continue using a faulty cable as it can damage your phone's charging port.

Q4: Can I return wireless earbuds if I find the sound quality "tinny" compared to my previous ones?
A: Sound quality preference is subjective and not a defect. Since in-ear earbuds are hygiene products, they are non-returnable once the seal is broken. Read expert reviews and frequency response descriptions before purchasing if sound signature is important to you.

Q5: I bought a power bank that's supposed to be 20,000mAh, but it only charges my 4,000mAh phone three times. Is it under-capacity?
A: Real-world capacity is always less than the rated capacity due to voltage conversion losses (typically 60-70% efficiency). A 20,000mAh power bank providing roughly 12,000-14,000mAh of usable output is normal. Three full charges of a 4,000mAh phone (12,000mAh) is within the expected range. If you're getting significantly less, it may be defective. Report within 7 days for testing.

Q6: My phone mount's suction cup won't stick to my dashboard. I've cleaned both surfaces. Is it defective?
A: Some dashboard textures (leather grain, curved surfaces) are incompatible with suction mounts. Check if your dashboard material is listed as compatible. If the suction cup fails on a smooth, flat glass surface, it is defective. Report within 7 days with a video. A replacement will be provided.

Q7: I ordered a case for iPhone 15 Pro, but I received a case for iPhone 15. The packaging says 15 Pro, but the case doesn't fit. What do I do?
A: This is a packaging/labeling error resulting in a wrong product. Photograph the case on your phone showing the misalignment, and the packaging. Report within 7 days. The correct case will be shipped.

Q8: Can I return a memory card if my phone says it's corrupted right out of the package?
A: First, try formatting the card in a computer using the official SD Card Formatter tool. If it still fails or shows incorrect capacity (e.g., 128GB card showing 32GB), it's likely counterfeit or defective. Report within 7 days. A replacement will be shipped. Always buy memory cards from authorized sellers.

Q9: My Bluetooth earphones connect and disconnect repeatedly when my phone is in my pocket. Is this a defect?
A: Bluetooth range is affected by body interference. If the disconnection happens within 1-2 meters with direct line of sight, it's a defect. If it only happens when the phone is in a back pocket or across the body, it may be a range/body-blocking limitation. Test in open air. Report within 7 days with a video if the issue persists.

Q10: I bought a fast charger, but my phone isn't fast charging. The phone supports fast charging. What's wrong?
A: Fast charging requires compatibility between the charger's protocol (Quick Charge, USB-PD, VOOC, etc.) and the phone's supported protocol, AND a compatible cable. Check all three. If the charger is rated for your phone's protocol and still slow charges, report within 7 days. A replacement will be provided if confirmed defective.

Q11: Can I return a pop socket that I stuck on my phone and now it won't come off to return it?
A: No. Once applied, pop sockets and adhesive grips are non-returnable. Removing them often damages the adhesive, making them unusable for resale. Check placement and feel before applying the adhesive permanently.

Q12: My new earphones have no sound from the left earbud. I've tried multiple devices. Is it DOA?
A: Yes, a completely non-functional earbud out of the box is Dead on Arrival/defective. Report within 7 days with a video showing the issue across multiple devices. A replacement set will be shipped.

Q13: I received a tempered glass with a small crack in the corner inside the sealed package. Can I return it?
A: Yes. Photograph the cracked tempered glass while it's still in the sealed package, showing the crack clearly. Report within 7 days. A replacement will be shipped. Do not open or attempt to apply a cracked screen protector.

Q14: Can I return a phone case if the color is slightly different from the website photo?
A: Minor color variations due to screen calibration are not defects. If the color is fundamentally different (e.g., ordered navy blue, received bright red), it qualifies as "Wrong Product." Report within 7 days with a comparison photo.

Q15: My USB-C hub gets extremely hot when I use it with my laptop. Is this a fire hazard?
A: Some warmth is normal for powered hubs. However, if it becomes too hot to touch or emits a burning smell, unplug immediately. This is a safety defect. Report within 7 days with a description. A replacement will be provided. Do not continue using an overheating accessory.

Q16: I bought a smartwatch strap that's too short for my wrist. Can I exchange it?
A: Yes, if the strap is unworn, with tags, and within 7 days. Once worn, it's non-returnable for hygiene. Check the strap length (usually in mm) against your wrist size before ordering.

Q17: My selfie stick's Bluetooth shutter button doesn't pair with my phone. Is it defective?
A: Ensure the selfie stick is charged, in pairing mode, and your phone's Bluetooth is on. Check compatibility (some are iOS or Android specific). If it still doesn't pair, report within 7 days with a video. A replacement will be shipped.

Q18: Can I return a phone case that has sharp edges around the charging port cutout that hurt my finger?
A: Yes. A sharp edge on a case is a manufacturing defect (poor finishing). Report within 7 days with a photo showing the sharp edge. A replacement or refund will be provided.

Q19: I ordered a wireless charger, and it charges my phone very slowly — slower than the wired charger that came with the phone. Is this normal?
A: Yes. Wireless charging is inherently slower than wired charging for most phones. The charging speed (5W, 7.5W, 10W, 15W) is listed in the specifications. If it's charging at the rated wattage, it's functioning correctly. "Slow" wireless charging is not a defect.

Q20: My phone case's design/print is peeling off after only a week of use. Is this covered?
A: Yes. Premature peeling of the design/print indicates poor adhesion and a manufacturing defect. Report within 7 days with photos. A replacement case will be provided.

Q21: I bought a car charger, and it doesn't fit in my car's 12V socket properly. It's loose and falls out. Is it defective?
A: If the charger is loose in multiple vehicles' sockets, it's a sizing defect. If it's only loose in your specific car, the car's socket may be worn. Report within 7 days with a video. We'll replace it once; if the new one also doesn't fit, the issue is likely with your car's socket.

Q22: Can I return an OTG adapter if my phone doesn't support OTG?
A: No. OTG compatibility is device-dependent. It is the customer's responsibility to check if their phone/tablet supports OTG before purchasing. An incompatible accessory is not defective.

Q23: My headphones have a constant static/buzzing sound when nothing is playing. Is this a defect?
A: A slight noise floor is normal, especially in wireless headphones. If the static is loud, distracting, or present even during playback, it's a defect. Report within 7 days with a recording. A replacement will be provided.

Q24: I bought a stylus pen for my tablet, but the palm rejection doesn't work. Is it the stylus or my tablet?
A: Palm rejection is a feature of the tablet and its software, not the stylus alone. Check if your tablet model supports palm rejection with third-party styluses. Many require proprietary styluses. This is a compatibility issue, not a stylus defect.

Q25: My phone ring holder's adhesive came off the phone after 3 days. The phone surface was clean. Can I return it?
A: Yes. Adhesive failure on a clean, smooth, non-porous surface within days indicates a defect. Report within 7 days with a photo. A replacement will be provided.

Q26: I received a charging cable that's only 50cm long, but the product page said 1 meter. What do I do?
A: Measure the cable and photograph it next to a measuring tape. Report within 7 days. A cable of incorrect length is a "Wrong Product" or specification error. The correct length cable will be shipped.

Q27: Can I return a phone case if the buttons are too stiff and hard to press?
A: Yes. Excessively stiff buttons that make the phone difficult to operate are a design/manufacturing defect. Report within 7 days with a description. If you've installed the case, photograph it. Cases with functional defects are accepted even if installed.

Q28: My power bank's USB port is loose, and the cable keeps falling out. Is this a defect?
A: Yes. A loose USB port that doesn't hold the cable securely is a manufacturing defect. Report within 7 days with a video showing the loose connection. A replacement power bank will be shipped.

Q29: I bought a "rugged" phone case, and it broke when my phone fell from waist height. The phone is fine, but the case is cracked. Is the case defective?
A: The case absorbed the impact and protected the phone — that's its job. Cases can crack while protecting the device, and this is not a defect. If the case shattered into pieces from a minor drop, it may be a quality issue. Report within 7 days with photos; these claims are assessed case by case.

Q30: My tempered glass screen protector has a rainbow/oil slick effect in the center. Is this a defect?
A: A rainbow or oil-slick effect (Newton's rings) occurs when the screen protector isn't fully adhered to the screen in that spot, creating a microscopic air gap. This is an application issue, not a product defect. It cannot be returned once applied. Some protectors have a dot matrix to prevent this; check product features before purchasing.
